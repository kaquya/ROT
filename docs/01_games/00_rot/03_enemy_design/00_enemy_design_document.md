# ROT – Enemy Design Document

Version: 1.0  
Status: Gameplay Design

---

# 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the enemy ecosystem encountered throughout ROT.

It describes:

• enemy categories  
• behavioral roles within the Rot ecosystem  
• combat characteristics  
• encounter design philosophy  
• progression of enemy difficulty

Enemies in ROT should feel like **biological organisms produced by an evolving ecosystem**, not just monsters placed for combat.

Each enemy type should appear to serve a **functional ecological role** within the Rot network.

---

# 2. Enemy Design Philosophy

Enemy design follows several key principles.

---

## Biological Plausibility

Enemies should appear as distorted reconstructions of real organisms.

Mutations should reflect:

• biological instability  
• incomplete anatomical reconstruction  
• fusion of multiple biological structures

Creatures should look like **failed attempts at rebuilding life**.

---

## Ecological Roles

Every enemy serves a role within the Rot ecosystem.

Examples include:

• scavengers  
• predators  
• guardians of Rot growth  
• biomass recyclers

This reinforces the idea that the Rot is forming a functioning ecosystem.

---

## Encounter Variety

Enemy encounters should vary based on environment.

Examples:

Indoor environments

• ambush enemies  
• narrow corridor encounters

Outdoor environments

• roaming predators  
• multi-enemy engagements

Variety prevents combat from becoming predictable.

---

# 3. Enemy Categories

Enemy types are divided into several major categories.

---

# 3.1 Rotters

Rotters are the most common enemies encountered in the game.

They originate from infected human hosts.

---

## Physical Characteristics

Rotters retain recognizable human anatomy but show severe biological distortion.

Typical features include:

• uneven limb growth  
• exposed bone structures  
• distorted posture  
• abnormal skin textures

These organisms appear unstable and partially reconstructed.

---

## Behavior

Rotters exhibit simple behavior patterns.

They typically:

• wander slowly  
• respond to sound or movement  
• attack by lunging or grabbing

Rotters are individually weak but dangerous in groups.

---

## Gameplay Role

Rotters serve as the baseline enemy type.

They introduce players to:

• combat mechanics  
• stealth avoidance  
• environmental awareness

---

# 3.2 Collapsed Rotters

Collapsed Rotters represent a later stage of human mutation.

Their skeletal structure has partially collapsed under Rot growth.

---

## Physical Characteristics

These creatures appear severely deformed.

Features include:

• crawling movement  
• twisted limbs  
• partially fused torsos

They often appear in dark interior environments.

---

## Behavior

Collapsed Rotters rely on ambush tactics.

They remain dormant until the player approaches.

When activated they:

• crawl rapidly toward the player  
• attempt close-range attacks

---

## Gameplay Role

Collapsed Rotters increase tension in confined spaces.

They punish players who move too quickly through dark environments.

---

# 3.3 Harbor Rotters

Harbor Rotters are Rot organisms adapted to marine environments.

They appear primarily in the harbor district.

---

## Physical Characteristics

These creatures show integration with marine life.

Features may include:

• barnacle-like growths  
• shell fragments embedded in flesh  
• elongated limbs resembling marine appendages

They appear heavier and more durable than normal Rotters.

---

## Behavior

Harbor Rotters move more slowly but absorb more damage.

They may:

• block narrow pathways  
• guard key locations  
• force players into tactical combat situations

---

## Gameplay Role

Harbor Rotters introduce tougher enemies that require careful resource management.

---

# 3.4 Medical Rotters

Medical Rotters originate from infected hospital patients.

Experimental treatment attempts created unstable mutation patterns.

---

## Physical Characteristics

Their bodies show signs of medical intervention.

Features may include:

• surgical scars  
• exposed bone plates  
• irregular limb elongation

These creatures often appear in hospital corridors.

---

## Behavior

Medical Rotters show erratic movement patterns.

They may:

• move unpredictably  
• twitch or collapse temporarily  
• lunge suddenly at the player

---

## Gameplay Role

Medical Rotters increase unpredictability during encounters.

---

# 3.5 Stalkers

Stalkers represent predator organisms within the Rot ecosystem.

Unlike Rotters, which behave erratically, Stalkers display deliberate hunting behavior.

They are significantly faster and more dangerous than basic enemies.

---

## Physical Characteristics

Stalkers appear more structurally stable than other Rot organisms.

Their bodies show:

• elongated limbs for rapid movement  
• reinforced skeletal structures  
• reduced visible decay compared to Rotters

Their anatomy appears optimized for hunting.

---

## Behavior

Stalkers actively track the player.

Typical behavior includes:

• stalking from shadows  
• circling around obstacles  
• ambushing from blind angles

Stalkers rarely approach directly unless the player is exposed.

---

## Variants

Two primary variants exist.

Shadow Stalkers

These creatures prefer dark interior environments such as warehouses and hospital corridors.

Hunter Stalkers

These variants patrol outdoor areas and forest environments.

---

## Gameplay Role

Stalkers create high-tension encounters.

Players must rely on stealth and environmental awareness to avoid them.

Direct combat is possible but risky.

---

# 3.6 Rot Wildlife

The Rot ecosystem affects animals as well as humans.

Mutated wildlife introduces different movement patterns and attack behaviors.

---

## Rot Wolves

These creatures appear in the forest region.

Features include:

• skeletal distortions  
• exposed muscle structures  
• aggressive pack behavior

They attack quickly and may surround the player.

---

## Rot Stag

The Rot Stag is a large mutated deer acting as a territorial predator.

Features include:

• antlers fused with Rot growth structures  
• powerful charge attacks  
• high mobility

The Rot Stag serves as a mini-boss encounter in the forest region.

---

# 3.7 Spliced Organisms

Spliced organisms form when the Rot merges multiple hosts into a single structure.

These creatures are significantly larger and more durable than standard enemies.

---

## Physical Characteristics

Spliced organisms appear grotesquely fused.

Features may include:

• multiple torsos merged together  
• irregular limb placement  
• exposed Rot biomass structures

Their bodies appear unstable yet powerful.

---

## Behavior

Spliced creatures move slowly but deal devastating damage.

They often:

• guard important areas  
• block progression routes  
• require heavy resource use to defeat

---

## Gameplay Role

Spliced enemies function as elite threats.

Players must decide whether to:

• fight  
• avoid  
• return later when better equipped

---

# 4. Mini-Boss Creatures

Mini-bosses represent apex organisms within localized Rot ecosystems.

Unlike normal enemies, these creatures control specific territories.

---

## The Caretaker

Location

Residential District

Description

A large fused organism formed from multiple infected residents.

Behavior

• sweeping limb attacks  
• close-range grappling  
• Rot spore bursts

Reward

Inventory expansion item.

---

## Rot Stag

Location

Forest Region

Description

A mutated deer integrated deeply into the Rot ecosystem.

Behavior

• high-speed charge attacks  
• stomp shockwaves  
• territorial aggression

Reward

Rare crafting material.

---

## Ward Mother

Location

Hospital District

Description

An organism formed when multiple infected patients merged during quarantine.

Behavior

• spawning smaller Rot organisms  
• slow pursuit  
• wide attack sweeps

Reward

Hospital master key.

---

## Prototype Subject

Location

Halcyon Research Center

Description

A failed experiment attempting to accelerate Rot integration in human hosts.

Behavior

• rapid lunging attacks  
• erratic movement patterns  
• climbing walls

Reward

Executive security keycard.

---

# 5. Enemy Difficulty Progression

Enemy difficulty increases as the Rot ecosystem becomes more complex.

---

## Early Game

Primary enemies:

• Rotters  
• Collapsed Rotters

Encounters are relatively small.

The player learns basic survival mechanics.

---

## Mid Game

Additional enemies appear:

• Harbor Rotters  
• Medical Rotters  
• Stalkers

Encounters become more dangerous and unpredictable.

---

## Late Game

Advanced enemies appear:

• Spliced organisms  
• Rot wildlife  
• multiple enemy types per encounter

Players must combine combat, stealth, and puzzle solving to survive.

---

# 6. Encounter Design Principles

Enemy placement should follow several rules.

---

## Environmental Context

Enemies should appear in environments that match their biological origin.

Examples:

Harbor Rotters in marine environments.

Medical Rotters in hospital areas.

---

## Encounter Variety

Encounters should vary between:

• single powerful enemies  
• small groups of weaker enemies  
• ambush situations

---

## Resource Pressure

Enemy encounters should create pressure on the player's resources.

Players should rarely feel fully stocked with supplies.

---

# 7. Enemy Design Goals

Enemies in ROT should reinforce the core themes of the game.

They must:

• reflect the biological instability of the Rot ecosystem  
• create tension during exploration  
• encourage tactical decision making  
• support environmental storytelling

Enemies should feel like **living components of a corrupted ecosystem rather than generic monsters**.
