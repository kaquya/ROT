# 1. Overview

The Resource Economy defines how items, supplies, and crafting materials are distributed throughout the world of ROT.

Resource management is a central component of the survival horror experience. Players must constantly evaluate whether to fight enemies, avoid encounters, or conserve supplies for future situations.

The goal of the resource economy is to maintain tension by ensuring that resources are valuable and limited while still allowing players to survive through careful exploration and decision making.

This document defines the principles governing item distribution, scarcity, and resource balance.

---

# 2. Resource Economy Philosophy

The resource economy is designed to reinforce survival gameplay.

Key principles include:

- scarcity creates tension  
- exploration rewards preparation  
- risk creates opportunity  
- resources should feel valuable  

Players should rarely feel fully prepared for every encounter.

Instead, survival depends on managing limited supplies.

---

# 3. Resource Categories

Resources in ROT fall into several main categories.

---

## Ammunition

Ammunition supports ranged combat weapons.

Examples include:

- handgun ammunition  
- shotgun shells  
- specialized rounds

Ammunition should remain limited to prevent players from relying exclusively on firearms.

Players are encouraged to conserve ammunition for dangerous encounters.

---

## Medical Supplies

Medical supplies restore player health.

Examples include:

- bandages  
- first aid kits  
- emergency treatment packs

Healing resources should be valuable but not extremely rare.

Players should feel pressure when injured but still have opportunities to recover.

---

## Crafting Materials

Crafting materials are used to create survival items.

Examples include:

- scrap metal  
- chemicals  
- mechanical parts  
- biological samples

These materials allow players to create useful equipment through the crafting system.

---

## Utility Resources

Utility resources support exploration and environmental interaction.

Examples include:

- batteries  
- tool components  
- fuel canisters  
- repair materials

These items may enable access to new areas or activate environmental systems.

---

## Key Progression Items

Some items are tied directly to world progression.

Examples include:

- access cards  
- mechanical keys  
- security overrides  
- specialized equipment

These items unlock new areas or puzzle systems.

---

# 4. Resource Distribution

Resources should be distributed throughout the environment in believable ways.

Items should appear in locations where they logically belong.

Examples include:

- medical supplies in hospitals  
- tools in industrial areas  
- ammunition in security offices  
- research materials in laboratories

Logical placement strengthens environmental storytelling.

---

# 5. Exploration Rewards

Players who thoroughly explore the environment should find additional resources.

Examples include:

- hidden rooms  
- locked containers  
- puzzle reward areas  
- elevated or difficult to reach locations

These rewards encourage exploration and curiosity.

---

# 6. Risk-Based Resource Placement

Some valuable resources should be placed in dangerous environments.

Examples include:

- heavily infected buildings  
- enemy occupied areas  
- contaminated Rot growth zones

Players must decide whether the potential reward justifies the risk.

---

# 7. Resource Scarcity Balance

Scarcity should be carefully balanced.

Resources should be rare enough to create tension but not so rare that players become unable to progress.

Balancing considerations include:

- average ammunition consumption per encounter  
- healing item usage during exploration  
- crafting material availability

Playtesting is required to determine appropriate resource levels.

---

# 8. Resource Flow Through the Game

Resource availability may change as the player progresses through the game.

Early game areas provide limited but sufficient supplies to teach resource management.

Mid game areas introduce more complex resource decisions.

Late game areas may contain higher value resources but also greater risks.

This progression reinforces the escalating danger of the Rot ecosystem.

---

# 9. Integration with Other Systems

The Resource Economy interacts with several gameplay systems.

Examples include:

- Inventory System for item storage  
- Crafting System for item creation  
- Combat System for ammunition usage  
- Difficulty Balance System for resource tuning

Balancing changes should always consider how these systems interact.

---

# 10. Purpose of This Document

This document defines how resources are distributed and balanced throughout ROT.

By maintaining scarcity, rewarding exploration, and encouraging strategic decision making, the resource economy reinforces the survival horror identity of the game.
