# Enemy Statistics

This document defines the **baseline combat statistics** for enemy types encountered in ROT.

These values represent **prototype balance targets** used during early development.

Exact numbers may change during playtesting and balancing.

Enemy statistics define:

- health
- damage
- movement speed
- attack behavior
- difficulty tier

These values are used by the combat system, AI system, and encounter design.

---

# Enemy Design Philosophy

Enemies in ROT are designed to create tension and force the player to make decisions.

Key principles:

• enemies are dangerous even in small numbers  
• combat should consume valuable resources  
• stealth and avoidance are valid strategies  
• stronger enemies require planning to defeat  

Enemies should rarely feel trivial.

---

# Enemy Difficulty Tiers

Enemy types are divided into several threat tiers.

Tier 1 — Common Infected  
Tier 2 — Advanced Predators  
Tier 3 — Elite Mutations  
Tier 4 — Boss Entities  

Each tier represents increasing danger.

---

# Tier 1 Enemies — Common Infected

These enemies represent the most common infected creatures.

They appear frequently throughout the game.

---

## Rotter

Rotters are infected humans whose bodies have begun mutating under Rot infection.

They are aggressive but not highly intelligent.

### Statistics

Health: 100  
Damage: 20  
Movement Speed: Slow  

### Behavior

- Wanders when idle
- Investigates sounds
- Attacks in groups
- Weak to headshots

### Threat Level

Low individually, moderate in groups.

---

## Collapsed Rotter

Collapsed Rotters represent a later stage of infection.

Their bodies are heavily deformed and unstable.

### Statistics

Health: 150  
Damage: 25  
Movement Speed: Very Slow  

### Behavior

- Limited mobility
- Lunging attacks
- May surprise the player when approached

### Threat Level

Low mobility but dangerous in confined spaces.

---

# Tier 2 Enemies — Predators

Predator-type enemies actively hunt the player.

They are more intelligent and more dangerous.

---

## Shadow Stalker

Shadow Stalkers are mutated infected that retain partial awareness.

They prefer dark environments.

### Statistics

Health: 140  
Damage: 35  
Movement Speed: Fast  

### Behavior

- hides in shadows
- ambush attacks
- retreats and repositions

### Threat Level

High.

Encounters should create strong tension.

---

## Hunter Stalker

A more aggressive variant of the Stalker.

Often found in outdoor environments.

### Statistics

Health: 160  
Damage: 40  
Movement Speed: Very Fast  

### Behavior

- actively tracks the player
- charges aggressively
- difficult to escape in open areas

### Threat Level

Very high.

Encounters often require strategic positioning.

---

# Tier 3 Enemies — Elite Mutations

Elite enemies are powerful mutated creatures created by advanced Rot infection.

These enemies are rare but extremely dangerous.

---

## Spliced

Spliced enemies are humans whose bodies have fused with Rot growth.

Their bodies become heavily mutated and distorted.

### Statistics

Health: 300  
Damage: 50  
Movement Speed: Medium  

### Behavior

- aggressive melee attacks
- high durability
- resistant to weak weapons

### Threat Level

Extremely high.

Encounters often function like mini-boss battles.

---

# Wildlife Mutations

The Rot also infects animals.

These enemies often appear in outdoor environments.

---

## Rot Wolf

Rot Wolves are infected predators that move quickly and attack in packs.

### Statistics

Health: 120  
Damage: 30  
Movement Speed: Fast  

### Behavior

- pack attacks
- charge attacks
- retreat and reposition

### Threat Level

Moderate individually, high in packs.

---

## Rot Stag (Mini Boss)

The Rot Stag is a heavily mutated deer infected by the Rot.

This creature serves as a mini-boss encounter.

### Statistics

Health: 500  
Damage: 60  
Movement Speed: Fast  

### Behavior

- high speed charge attacks
- stomp attacks
- retreats and repositions

### Threat Level

Very high.

---

# Boss Entities

Boss enemies represent large Rot mutations tied to major story events.

Boss encounters are multi-phase fights.

---

## Bloom Guardian

The Bloom Guardian forms when environmental Rot growth reaches extreme levels.

It merges biological material with surrounding structures.

### Statistics

Health: 1200  
Damage: 70  
Movement Speed: Slow  

### Behavior

- sweeping tentacle attacks
- spawns lesser Rot creatures
- environmental hazards

### Threat Level

Boss encounter.

---

## Rot Core Entity

The Rot Core represents the central organism responsible for the outbreak.

This entity forms during the final stage of the game.

### Statistics

Health: 2000  
Damage: 80  
Movement Speed: Variable  

### Behavior

- multi-phase attacks
- environmental corruption
- spawn waves of enemies

### Threat Level

Final boss.

---

# Enemy Weak Points

Many enemies have weak points.

Examples include:

- head
- exposed Rot growth
- mutated limbs

Targeting weak points increases damage.

This encourages precise combat.

---

# Enemy Scaling

Enemy difficulty increases as the game progresses.

Scaling methods include:

- stronger enemy variants
- increased enemy density
- more aggressive AI behavior

Scaling ensures that the player remains challenged throughout the game.

---

# Balancing Notes

These values represent **prototype statistics**.

Final values will be adjusted during:

- combat testing
- difficulty tuning
- playtesting sessions

Balancing aims to maintain:

- tension
- fairness
- strategic combat choices
