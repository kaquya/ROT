## 1. Overview

The Crafting System allows players to create useful items from resources found during exploration. Crafting provides an alternative way to obtain supplies when scavenging alone is not sufficient.

Crafting in ROT is intentionally limited and grounded in survival logic. It is not intended to function as a complex production system. Instead, crafting allows players to assemble practical survival tools from materials found in the environment.

Crafting supports the survival gameplay loop by allowing players to convert scavenged resources into essential equipment.

---

## 2. Crafting Philosophy

Crafting in ROT follows several design principles.

### Survival Focus

Crafting recipes should produce items that support survival and exploration rather than large scale equipment upgrades.

Examples include medical supplies, simple tools, or improvised ammunition.

### Limited Complexity

Crafting should remain simple and accessible. Recipes should use a small number of components and be easy for players to understand.

### Resource Scarcity

Crafting resources are limited and must be gathered through exploration. This ensures that crafting decisions remain meaningful.

### Environmental Logic

Crafted items should make sense within the world. Recipes should reflect objects that could plausibly be assembled from available materials.

---

## 3. Crafting Resources

Crafting resources are materials collected during exploration.

These materials are stored in the player inventory and used when crafting items.

Examples of crafting resources include:

- metal fragments  
- chemical compounds  
- medical components  
- mechanical parts  
- biological samples  

Resources may be found in abandoned buildings, research facilities, industrial areas, or contaminated environments.

Resource distribution should reflect the location where the materials originate.

---

## 4. Crafting Recipes

Crafting recipes define how resources can be combined to produce new items.

Each recipe specifies:

- required materials  
- quantity of materials needed  
- resulting crafted item  

Recipes may produce items such as:

- medical kits  
- improvised ammunition  
- basic tools  
- protective equipment components  

Recipes should remain limited to items that support survival and exploration.

---

## 5. Recipe Discovery

Players may begin the game with knowledge of basic crafting recipes.

Additional recipes may be discovered through exploration.

Examples of recipe discovery include:

- research documents found in laboratories  
- notes left by previous survivors  
- instruction manuals located in industrial environments  

Recipe discovery encourages exploration and narrative discovery.

---

## 6. Crafting Stations

Certain crafting actions require access to specialized equipment.

Crafting stations represent locations where players can assemble more complex items.

Examples of crafting stations include:

- workbenches in abandoned buildings  
- laboratory equipment in research facilities  
- maintenance areas within industrial structures  

Some simple items may be crafted directly from the player inventory, while more complex items require access to a crafting station.

---

## 7. Crafting Process

The crafting process typically follows several steps.

1. The player gathers crafting resources during exploration.  
2. The player accesses the crafting interface.  
3. Available recipes are displayed based on known blueprints.  
4. The player selects a recipe.  
5. Required materials are consumed and the crafted item is added to the inventory.

The process should remain quick and intuitive.

---

## 8. Crafting Limitations

Crafting should not replace scavenging as the primary method of obtaining resources.

To maintain survival tension:

- crafting materials should remain limited  
- powerful items should remain rare  
- crafting should not allow players to bypass progression systems  

Crafting is intended to supplement exploration rather than dominate gameplay.

---

## 9. Integration with Other Systems

The Crafting System interacts with several gameplay systems.

Examples include:

- the Inventory System for storing resources and crafted items  
- the Exploration System for discovering crafting materials  
- the Progression System for unlocking new recipes  
- the Interaction System for accessing crafting stations  

These connections ensure that crafting remains integrated with the overall gameplay experience.

---

## 10. Purpose of This Document

This document defines how crafting functions in ROT.

It establishes the rules for crafting resources, recipes, and crafting stations.

Following these guidelines ensures that crafting supports the survival gameplay loop while remaining grounded in the realistic world of ROT.
