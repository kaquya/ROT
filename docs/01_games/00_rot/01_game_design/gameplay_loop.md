# Gameplay Loop

Project: ROT  
Version: 1.1  
Document Type: Game Design  
Category: Gameplay Structure  
Scope: ROT (Game)  
Status: Core Reference  

---

## 1. Overview

The gameplay loop of ROT describes the repeating cycle of actions that players perform throughout the game. This loop defines how exploration, survival, and narrative discovery interact with each other to create the core experience.

ROT is built around a loop that encourages careful investigation of environments while managing danger and limited resources.

The main gameplay loop can be summarized as:

explore → scavenge → survive → uncover story → progress

This loop repeats continuously as players move deeper into the Rot affected regions of Blackwater Cove.

---

## 2. Explore

Exploration is the entry point of the gameplay loop.

Players navigate interconnected environments in order to locate resources, discover narrative clues, and identify new routes through the world.

Exploration includes:

- investigating buildings and interior spaces  
- navigating streets, infrastructure, and natural environments  
- observing environmental clues and biological changes  
- locating locked paths or environmental barriers  

The world is designed to reward curiosity and careful observation.

Exploration often reveals optional areas that contain additional resources or narrative information.

---

## 3. Scavenge

While exploring, players search for useful items that support survival.

Scavenging provides essential resources required to continue exploring dangerous areas.

Common scavenged resources include:

- ammunition  
- medical supplies  
- crafting materials  
- tools or keys required to unlock new areas  

Resource availability is intentionally limited. This forces players to make careful decisions about how and when resources should be used.

---

## 4. Survive

Encounters with Rot organisms and environmental hazards introduce survival challenges.

Players must decide how to handle threats encountered during exploration.

Possible survival strategies include:

- avoiding creatures through stealth  
- escaping dangerous encounters  
- using limited combat options  
- navigating environmental hazards carefully  

Survival situations are intended to create tension and force players to adapt to the environment.

---

## 5. Uncover Story

As players explore and scavenge, they discover fragments of the narrative.

Story elements are delivered through environmental storytelling and research materials left behind in the world.

Examples include:

- research logs describing Rot experiments  
- personal notes from former residents  
- abandoned laboratories and containment areas  
- environmental scenes that reveal past events  

These discoveries help players understand what happened in Blackwater Cove and how the Rot transformed the region.

---

## 6. Progress

Progress occurs when players gain access to new locations or uncover key information about the environment.

Progression may involve:

- unlocking previously inaccessible areas  
- obtaining tools or keys that allow access to new districts  
- understanding environmental hazards and how to navigate them  
- discovering narrative information that guides the next objective  

Each progression step expands the playable world and deepens the player's understanding of the Rot ecosystem.

---

## 7. Loop Reinforcement

The gameplay loop reinforces itself as players move deeper into the world.

New areas introduce:

- more complex environments  
- increased creature activity  
- stronger ecological transformation  
- additional narrative discoveries  

These changes maintain player engagement while gradually increasing tension and difficulty.

---

## 8. Loop Evolution

Although the core loop remains consistent, the experience evolves throughout the game.

Early areas focus on exploration and basic survival.

Mid game areas introduce more frequent encounters and larger contaminated environments.

Late game areas present heavily transformed ecosystems and the most dangerous threats.

The loop remains the same, but the stakes and complexity increase.

---

## 9. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the core gameplay loop that drives the ROT experience.

All gameplay systems, level design decisions, and progression mechanics should support and reinforce this loop.

Maintaining a clear and consistent gameplay loop ensures that the player experience remains focused and engaging throughout the game.
