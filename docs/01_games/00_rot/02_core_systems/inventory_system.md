## 1. Overview

The Inventory System defines how the player stores, manages, and uses items during gameplay.

Inventory management is an important part of the survival horror experience in ROT. Resources are intentionally limited, and players must make careful decisions about what items to carry and when to use them.

The inventory system supports three primary functions:

- item storage  
- equipment management  
- resource decision making  

Players should frequently evaluate their inventory and adjust their strategy depending on the resources available.

---

## 2. Inventory Philosophy

The inventory system follows several core principles.

### Limited Capacity

Players cannot carry unlimited items. Inventory space is restricted to encourage meaningful decisions about which resources are worth carrying.

### Clear Organization

Items should be organized in a way that allows players to quickly understand what they are carrying.

### Strategic Resource Use

Players should be forced to decide when to consume or conserve items such as ammunition, medical supplies, and tools.

### Minimal Disruption

Inventory management should remain simple and efficient so that players can quickly return to gameplay.

---

## 3. Item Storage

Item storage determines how many items the player can carry and how they are organized.

### Inventory Capacity

The player inventory has a limited number of slots. Each slot can contain one item stack or one piece of equipment.

Capacity limitations force players to prioritize important resources.

### Item Categories

Items are divided into several categories.

Examples include:

- ammunition  
- medical supplies  
- crafting materials  
- tools  
- keys and progression items  

Categorization helps players quickly identify items within the inventory.

### Stackable Items

Certain items can be stacked within a single slot.

Examples include:

- ammunition  
- crafting materials  

Stacking helps reduce inventory clutter while maintaining capacity limits.

---

## 4. Equipment Slots

Equipment slots represent items that the player actively carries or uses.

These slots allow players to access important equipment quickly without opening the full inventory.

Typical equipment slots may include:

- primary weapon slot  
- secondary weapon slot  
- tool slot  
- equipment slot for protective gear  

Equipment slots allow fast switching between commonly used items during gameplay.

---

## 5. Item Management

Players can manage items within the inventory through several actions.

### Use Item

Consumable items such as medical supplies or certain tools can be used directly from the inventory.

Using an item removes it from the inventory and applies its effect.

### Equip Item

Weapons or tools can be moved into equipment slots.

Equipped items become available for immediate use during gameplay.

### Discard Item

Players may choose to discard items if inventory space becomes limited.

Discarded items are removed from the player's inventory and cannot be recovered.

### Rearrange Items

Players can rearrange items within the inventory to maintain organization and prioritize important resources.

---

## 6. Inventory Access

Players can open the inventory through the game interface.

Opening the inventory pauses or slows gameplay depending on the design choice for the game mode.

The interface should display:

- current inventory slots  
- equipped items  
- item descriptions  
- item quantities  

This allows players to evaluate their resources and make decisions about item usage.

---

## 7. Progression and Inventory Expansion

As the game progresses, the player may gain access to improved storage capacity or specialized equipment.

Examples include:

- additional inventory slots  
- improved equipment containers  
- specialized gear that occupies separate equipment slots  

Inventory expansion should remain limited to maintain the survival tension of resource management.

---

## 8. Integration with Other Systems

The Inventory System interacts with several other gameplay systems.

Examples include:

- the Combat System for weapon and ammunition management  
- the Crafting System for resource usage  
- the Interaction System for item pickup and usage  
- the Player Progression System for equipment upgrades  

These integrations ensure that inventory management plays an important role in the survival experience.

---

## 9. Purpose of This Document

This document defines how items are stored, equipped, and managed in ROT.

The Inventory System ensures that resource management remains an important part of gameplay while allowing players to organize and access their equipment efficiently.

Maintaining clear inventory rules helps support the survival horror identity of the game.
