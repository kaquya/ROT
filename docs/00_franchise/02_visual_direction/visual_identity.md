## 1. Overview

The visual identity of the ROT franchise defines how the world should appear across all projects within the universe. It establishes a consistent visual language that reflects the themes of biological transformation, ecological takeover, and the slow decay of human environments.

ROT environments should feel grounded, natural, and believable while simultaneously unsettling. The visual style should communicate that the world is undergoing a biological restructuring process driven by the Rot.

The goal of this document is to ensure that artists, designers, and developers maintain a consistent visual tone when creating environments, creatures, and atmospheric elements.

---

## 2. Core Visual Principles

The visual identity of ROT is guided by several core principles.

### Biological Transformation

The world should visually communicate that biological growth is reshaping environments.

Organic structures should appear to grow through or around human architecture. Mutated organisms should appear as natural extensions of their environment rather than artificial or monstrous additions.

The visual result should suggest that life itself is reorganizing the world.

### Environmental Decay

Human environments should show clear signs of abandonment and degradation.

Examples include:

- weathered buildings
- rusted infrastructure
- collapsed structures
- overgrown vegetation

Decay should appear gradual and believable.

### Naturalistic Horror

Horror should emerge from realistic biological and environmental changes.

Visual horror should not rely on exaggerated or stylized elements. Instead, it should focus on unsettling organic transformations that feel plausible within the ecosystem.

### Environmental Integration

Mutated organisms and Rot growth should visually integrate with the environment.

Creatures, plants, and environmental structures should appear connected as part of the same ecosystem.

---

## 3. Color Palette

The ROT universe uses a restrained and natural color palette to reinforce its grounded tone.

Primary colors should be derived from natural environments and biological materials.

Typical palette elements include:

- muted greens representing vegetation and organic growth
- dark browns and soil tones representing decay and earth
- desaturated blues and greys representing coastal environments and weather
- pale organic colors such as bone, fungus, or rot tissue

Bright or highly saturated colors should be used sparingly.

Occasional unnatural colors may appear in specific Rot growths or biological structures to emphasize mutation, but these should remain rare and purposeful.

---

## 4. Lighting Tone

Lighting in ROT environments should emphasize mood, atmosphere, and uncertainty.

Most environments should use subdued lighting that reinforces tension and exploration.

Common lighting characteristics include:

- overcast skies
- low sunlight conditions
- soft ambient lighting
- heavy shadow areas
- atmospheric fog or mist

Interior environments should rely on limited light sources such as:

- emergency lighting
- damaged electrical systems
- small portable lights

Lighting should guide the player’s attention while preserving areas of visual ambiguity.

---

## 5. Texture Philosophy

Textures within the ROT universe should emphasize realism and material aging.

Surfaces should reflect environmental wear and biological integration.

Common texture characteristics include:

- cracked concrete
- rusted metal
- peeling paint
- water damage
- organic growth spreading across surfaces

Rot affected textures should combine biological and structural materials. For example, fungal or plant-like growth may merge with metal, wood, or stone surfaces.

The goal is to visually communicate that the environment itself is being absorbed into a living ecosystem.

---

## 6. Environmental Atmosphere

ROT environments should feel quiet and heavy with atmosphere.

Visual composition should prioritize:

- dense environmental detail
- layered environmental storytelling
- partially obscured spaces
- environmental clutter and debris

Abandoned objects, damaged infrastructure, and biological growth should contribute to the sense that environments have evolved beyond human control.

Atmospheric elements such as fog, drifting particles, and slow organic movement can reinforce this effect.

---

## 7. Visual Horror Presentation

Horror in ROT should be subtle and grounded in environmental realism.

Rather than relying on sudden visual shock, environments should gradually reveal unsettling details.

Examples include:

- mutated organisms partially hidden in foliage
- biological growth embedded in building interiors
- skeletal remains integrated into Rot structures
- distorted anatomy that appears functional rather than grotesque

The most effective visual horror moments occur when players recognize familiar forms that have been transformed by the Rot ecosystem.

---

## 8. Consistency Across Projects

All projects within the ROT franchise should follow the visual guidelines described in this document.

While individual environments may vary depending on location or story context, the overall visual identity must remain consistent.

This ensures that players can immediately recognize environments within the ROT universe through their visual tone, environmental detail, and biological integration.

---

## 9. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the visual identity of the ROT franchise.

It provides artists and designers with a clear framework for creating environments, creatures, and atmospheric elements that align with the themes of ecological horror and biological transformation.

Maintaining a consistent visual identity ensures that all ROT projects share a recognizable artistic direction.
