# 1. Overview

The Collectibles System defines how narrative fragments and optional discoveries are distributed throughout the world of ROT.

Collectibles provide players with deeper insight into the story, characters, and events surrounding the Rot outbreak. They serve as a method of environmental storytelling that encourages exploration without interrupting gameplay.

Rather than delivering the narrative through traditional exposition, collectibles allow players to uncover the history of Blackwater Cove gradually through documents, recordings, and personal belongings.

Collectibles are optional but strongly reward thorough exploration.

---

# 2. Collectible Design Philosophy

Collectibles follow several guiding principles.

## Narrative Discovery

Collectibles provide fragments of information rather than complete explanations. Players piece together the larger story by discovering multiple fragments across the world.

This approach reinforces the mystery surrounding the Rot ecosystem and the events in Blackwater Cove.

---

## Environmental Context

Collectibles should appear in locations where they logically belong.

Examples include:

- personal journals inside homes  
- research notes in laboratories  
- emergency broadcasts in control rooms  
- medical records in hospital areas  

This strengthens immersion and supports environmental storytelling.

---

## Exploration Reward

Collectibles should reward players who explore carefully.

They are often placed in:

- hidden rooms  
- optional side areas  
- locked containers  
- elevated or hard to reach locations

Players who thoroughly investigate environments gain a deeper understanding of the narrative.

---

## Narrative Fragmentation

No single collectible should explain the entire story.

Instead, narrative information should be spread across multiple documents.

This encourages players to collect and interpret multiple pieces of information.

---

# 3. Collectible Categories

Collectibles are divided into several major categories.

---

## Personal Records

Personal records reveal the experiences of civilians during the outbreak.

Examples include:

- handwritten journals  
- letters  
- family notes  
- evacuation plans  

These documents focus on the human side of the disaster.

They help players understand how residents responded to the crisis.

---

## Official Reports

Official reports come from government agencies, emergency responders, and municipal authorities.

Examples include:

- evacuation instructions  
- quarantine notices  
- police reports  
- emergency broadcasts

These documents reveal how local authorities attempted to manage the outbreak.

---

## Halcyon Research Documents

Research documents originate from the Halcyon Initiative.

Examples include:

- biological research logs  
- experimental reports  
- containment procedures  
- internal communications

These collectibles reveal the scientific investigation of the Rot ecosystem.

They also expose internal conflict within the organization.

---

## Environmental Records

Environmental records describe unusual ecological events and environmental changes.

Examples include:

- wildlife observation notes  
- marine research data  
- geological survey reports

These collectibles help explain the biological nature of the Rot ecosystem.

---

## Survivor Messages

Survivor messages represent communication left behind by people who attempted to escape or survive the outbreak.

Examples include:

- radio recordings  
- warning messages  
- emergency graffiti  
- audio diaries

These fragments help create a sense of urgency and tragedy.

---

# 4. Narrative Fragment System

Collectibles should function as narrative fragments that gradually build a larger story.

Each category of collectibles contributes to a different perspective on the outbreak.

Examples include:

Civilian perspective  
Official response perspective  
Scientific research perspective  
Ecological transformation perspective

By collecting fragments from multiple sources, players can reconstruct the events surrounding the Rot outbreak.

---

# 5. Placement Rules

Collectible placement should follow consistent design rules.

---

## Logical Placement

Collectibles must appear in believable locations.

Examples include:

- research documents in laboratories  
- medical records in hospitals  
- personal letters inside homes

Items should not appear randomly without narrative context.

---

## Exploration Incentives

Some collectibles should be placed in optional areas that require exploration.

Examples include:

- hidden rooms  
- rooftop areas  
- underground maintenance corridors

This encourages players to explore environments thoroughly.

---

## Safe Placement

Collectibles should generally be placed in locations where the player has enough time to read or inspect them.

Examples include:

- safe rooms  
- quiet exploration areas  
- puzzle reward locations

Players should not be forced to read collectibles during active combat.

---

## Narrative Clusters

Certain collectibles may appear as part of narrative clusters.

A group of related documents in the same area may tell a small story.

Examples include:

- multiple research notes describing a failed experiment  
- evacuation messages from several residents within the same building

Clusters strengthen environmental storytelling.

---

# 6. Collectible Tracking

The game may track collected items through an internal archive system.

The archive allows players to:

- review previously discovered documents  
- track missing collectibles  
- revisit narrative fragments

This system supports players who want to explore the full story.

---

# 7. Integration with Other Systems

The Collectibles System interacts with several other gameplay systems.

Examples include:

- the Exploration System for world navigation  
- the Inventory System for collectible storage  
- the Narrative System for story progression  
- the Level Design System for collectible placement

These systems ensure collectibles remain integrated with gameplay.

---

# 8. Purpose of This Document

This document defines how collectibles function within ROT.

Collectibles allow players to discover the story of Blackwater Cove through exploration and observation. By distributing narrative fragments throughout the world, the game encourages curiosity while maintaining the mystery surrounding the Rot ecosystem.
