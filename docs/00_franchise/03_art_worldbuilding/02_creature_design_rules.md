# ROT – Creature Design Rules

Version: 1.0  
Status: Franchise Creature Design Standard

---

# 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the rules used to design all hostile organisms in the ROT franchise.

Creatures in ROT are not traditional monsters.

They are **biological reconstructions produced by the Rot ecosystem**.

These rules ensure that all creature designs remain consistent with the core biological logic of the Rot phenomenon.

The document establishes:

• mutation rules  
• anatomical design guidelines  
• silhouette design principles  
• behavioral influence from biological origins  
• creature hierarchy within the Rot ecosystem

Every enemy design across the franchise must follow these rules.

---

# 2. Creature Design Philosophy

ROT creatures should always reinforce the central themes of the franchise:

• biological reconstruction  
• ecological horror  
• environmental integration  
• anatomical imperfection

Creatures should appear disturbing not because they are supernatural, but because they are **biologically wrong**.

They should feel like organisms that were reconstructed using incomplete biological information.

---

# 3. Biological Origin

Every Rot creature must have a clear biological origin.

The Rot does not invent entirely new lifeforms.

Instead, it reconstructs organisms using biological patterns stored within its archive.

These patterns come from:

• humans  
• animals  
• plant structures  
• environmental biological material

Understanding the creature's original biological source helps guide its final appearance.

---

# 4. Mutation Principles

Rot mutation follows several visual principles.

These principles ensure creatures remain believable within the Rot ecosystem.

---

## 4.1 Anatomical Distortion

Rot organisms often display distorted anatomy.

Common distortion patterns include:

• elongated limbs  
• twisted skeletal structures  
• exposed tissue growth  
• misaligned joints

These distortions occur because the Rot reconstruction process is imperfect.

---

## 4.2 Asymmetry

Rot organisms should rarely be symmetrical.

Examples of asymmetry include:

• one limb significantly larger than the other  
• uneven skeletal alignment  
• irregular tissue growth

Asymmetry reinforces the idea that these creatures are unstable biological reconstructions.

---

## 4.3 Structural Overgrowth

The Rot frequently produces excessive tissue growth.

Examples include:

• large muscle clusters  
• expanded bone structures  
• thick tissue plating

These features may serve biological purposes such as protection or locomotion.

---

## 4.4 Tissue Exposure

Rot organisms often display exposed biological structures.

Examples include:

• visible muscle tissue  
• exposed veins  
• partially open body cavities

These features emphasize the biological nature of the Rot.

---

# 5. Creature Silhouette Design

Silhouette design is critical for gameplay clarity.

Enemies must remain recognizable even in low lighting.

Players should be able to identify enemy types quickly.

---

## 5.1 Distinct Silhouettes

Each enemy type should have a unique outline.

Examples include:

Rotters  
Unstable humanoid silhouette.

Stalkers  
Low predatory silhouette designed for ambush.

Spliced Organisms  
Large distorted mass silhouettes.

These silhouettes help players identify threats instantly.

---

## 5.2 Readability in Darkness

Because ROT environments often feature low lighting, enemy silhouettes must remain visible even in darkness.

Key silhouette elements should remain readable through:

• exaggerated limb shapes  
• distinctive posture  
• recognizable movement patterns

---

# 6. Movement Design

Movement patterns strongly influence creature identity.

Movement should reflect the creature’s biological structure.

---

## 6.1 Instability

Many Rot organisms move in unstable or unnatural ways.

Examples include:

• uneven walking patterns  
• sudden bursts of movement  
• twitching limbs

Instability reinforces the idea that the creature's body is not functioning normally.

---

## 6.2 Predator Movement

Predatory organisms may move more fluidly.

Examples include:

• low stalking posture  
• silent movement  
• sudden attack lunges

These creatures often rely on stealth and speed.

---

## 6.3 Weight and Momentum

Large creatures should appear heavy and powerful.

Examples include:

• slow but devastating attacks  
• environmental impact during movement  
• visible strain in movement

Weight adds tension to encounters with large enemies.

---

# 7. Environmental Integration

Some Rot creatures appear partially integrated into their environment.

Examples include:

• creatures emerging from walls  
• organisms partially fused with structures  
• enemies hiding within growth clusters

Environmental integration strengthens the idea that the Rot ecosystem is a living system.
