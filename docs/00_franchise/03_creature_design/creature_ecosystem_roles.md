## 1. Overview

Rot ecosystems are structured biological systems. Organisms within these environments occupy defined ecological roles that allow the ecosystem to sustain itself.

Creatures in the ROT universe should never appear as random monsters. Each organism exists as part of an ecosystem with a specific biological function.

These roles influence creature anatomy, behavior, environmental placement, and interaction with other organisms.

Understanding these roles allows designers to create ecosystems that feel believable and biologically coherent.

---

## 2. Ecosystem Structure

A Rot ecosystem typically contains several categories of organisms that support different layers of the biological hierarchy.

These categories are not rigid species classifications. Instead, they represent functional roles within the ecosystem.

Common roles include:

- scavengers  
- predators  
- parasites  
- environmental organisms  
- apex mutations  

Each category contributes to ecosystem stability and environmental transformation.

---

## 3. Scavengers

### Overview

Scavengers are organisms that consume organic waste, dead organisms, and discarded biological material.

They help recycle biological matter and maintain ecosystem balance.

### Characteristics

Scavenger organisms typically have the following traits:

- smaller body size  
- mobility across terrain or structures  
- opportunistic feeding behavior  
- strong sensory organs for locating organic material  

### Behavior

Scavengers rarely initiate combat with larger organisms. Instead, they gather around biological remains or follow predators to feed on leftovers.

They may appear in groups and can spread throughout environments quickly.

### Design Guidelines

Scavengers should appear biologically efficient rather than threatening.

Design features may include:

- thin or lightweight bodies  
- numerous small limbs or crawling appendages  
- sensory structures such as antennae or tendrils  

---

## 4. Predators

### Overview

Predators are organisms that actively hunt other creatures within the Rot ecosystem.

They maintain population balance by controlling the numbers of smaller organisms.

### Characteristics

Predatory creatures usually display adaptations that support hunting.

These may include:

- strong limbs or claws  
- enhanced sensory organs  
- fast movement or ambush capabilities  
- reinforced skeletal structures  

### Behavior

Predators may patrol territory, ambush prey, or stalk targets over distance.

Their behavior should communicate that they are adapted hunters within the ecosystem.

### Design Guidelines

Predator designs should emphasize functional anatomy.

Examples include:

- limbs built for rapid movement  
- sensory structures such as elongated heads or exposed sensory tissue  
- strong jaw or feeding structures  

---

## 5. Parasites

### Overview

Parasites depend on other organisms to survive.

They attach to hosts or integrate themselves into biological systems in order to extract nutrients.

### Characteristics

Parasitic organisms often appear smaller or partially embedded in their host.

Typical traits include:

- attachment structures such as hooks or tendrils  
- feeding appendages  
- limited mobility without a host  

### Behavior

Parasites may attach themselves to larger organisms or environmental structures.

Some parasites alter the behavior or physical structure of their host.

### Design Guidelines

Parasitic organisms should appear biologically integrated with their host.

Designs may include:

- partially merged anatomical structures  
- organic tubes or feeding extensions  
- biological membranes linking parasite and host  

---

## 6. Environmental Organisms

### Overview

Environmental organisms are biological structures that reshape the ecosystem itself.

They may appear as stationary or slow growing life forms that spread across terrain and structures.

### Characteristics

Environmental organisms typically display traits such as:

- large stationary biological masses  
- root like networks spreading through terrain  
- organic structures that integrate with architecture  

### Behavior

These organisms do not actively hunt prey. Instead, they influence the environment by expanding biological growth.

They may serve as nutrient processors or biological anchors for Rot ecosystems.

### Design Guidelines

Environmental organisms should appear deeply integrated into the environment.

Design features may include:

- branching root systems  
- layered biological membranes  
- structural growth merging with terrain  

---

## 7. Apex Mutations

### Overview

Apex mutations represent the dominant organisms within a Rot ecosystem.

These creatures often occupy the highest position in the ecological hierarchy.

They may control territory or shape the behavior of other organisms.

### Characteristics

Apex mutations typically possess the following traits:

- large body size  
- complex biological structures  
- powerful predatory capabilities  
- unique anatomical features  

### Behavior

Apex organisms often control territory or appear in central locations within Rot ecosystems.

They may represent the culmination of biological restructuring in that environment.

### Design Guidelines

Apex mutations should appear biologically complex and imposing.

Design elements may include:

- layered anatomical structures  
- multiple sensory organs  
- reinforced biological plating  
- large scale mutation patterns  

---

## 8. Ecosystem Interaction

The different creature roles interact to maintain ecosystem balance.

Examples of interactions include:

- predators hunting scavengers or smaller organisms  
- parasites attaching to predators or environmental organisms  
- scavengers feeding on biological remains left by predators  

These relationships help establish believable ecosystems.

Designers should consider how creatures interact with each other rather than designing organisms in isolation.

---

## 9. Use in Enemy Design

In gameplay design, these ecological roles help determine enemy behavior and placement.

Examples include:

- scavengers appearing in large numbers in contaminated areas  
- predators guarding territory or hunting prey  
- parasites emerging from infected hosts  
- apex mutations acting as major threats or bosses  

These roles provide a framework for creating enemy variety while maintaining ecological consistency.

---

## 10. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the ecological roles used when designing creatures within the ROT universe.

By categorizing organisms according to their function within the ecosystem, designers can ensure that creature populations feel biologically coherent.

Following these guidelines helps maintain the identity of the Rot ecosystem across all ROT projects.
