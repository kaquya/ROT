## 1. Overview

ROT is a narrative driven survival horror game set in the coastal town of Blackwater Cove. The game focuses on exploration, environmental storytelling, and survival within a region that has been partially transformed by a biological phenomenon known as the Rot.

The Rot restructures organisms and ecosystems rather than simply infecting them. As a result, the environment itself becomes part of the threat. Buildings, wildlife, and terrain gradually integrate into new biological systems.

Players explore abandoned locations, investigate the history of the outbreak, and survive encounters with organisms reshaped by the Rot ecosystem.

The experience prioritizes atmosphere, discovery, and careful decision making over fast paced action.

---

## 2. Genre

ROT belongs to the survival horror genre with strong emphasis on exploration and environmental storytelling.

The game combines several genre characteristics:

- survival horror tension
- exploration based progression
- environmental storytelling
- limited resource management
- investigative narrative structure

Players are not heavily armed combatants. They are survivors navigating an unstable ecosystem.

---

## 3. Design Philosophy

The design philosophy of ROT is built around several key ideas.

### Environmental Immersion

The world should feel believable and interconnected. Environments should reflect real world logic and gradually reveal the effects of the Rot.

Players should feel like they are exploring a real place that has been transformed over time.

### Player Vulnerability

Players should feel capable of surviving threats but never completely safe.

Combat is possible but dangerous. Avoidance and careful movement are often safer strategies than direct confrontation.

### Discovery Driven Progression

Progression occurs through exploration and discovery rather than linear objectives.

Players uncover narrative information, environmental clues, and new areas by investigating the world.

### Biological Consistency

Creatures and environments must follow the biological logic established by the Rot ecosystem.

Mutations and environmental changes should appear functional and integrated into the ecosystem.

## 4. Setting

The game takes place in Blackwater Cove, a coastal town that was once a center of fishing and maritime trade.

After unusual ecological anomalies began appearing in the surrounding region, scientific organizations established research facilities to investigate the phenomenon.

These investigations eventually revealed the presence of the Rot.

Attempts to study and contain the phenomenon led to environmental instability and the gradual transformation of local ecosystems.

Many parts of the town were evacuated as the Rot began spreading through surrounding areas.

Players explore districts of Blackwater Cove that range from abandoned residential neighborhoods to heavily transformed Rot ecosystems.

---

## 5. Core Gameplay Loop

The core gameplay loop of ROT revolves around exploration, survival, and discovery.

The loop can be described in the following stages.

### Exploration

Players move through environments searching for resources, narrative clues, and new pathways.

Exploration includes investigating buildings, navigating environmental hazards, and locating hidden areas.

### Resource Management

Players collect and manage limited resources such as ammunition, medical supplies, and tools.

Resource scarcity forces players to make careful decisions about when to engage threats and when to avoid them.

### Encounter

While exploring, players may encounter creatures shaped by the Rot ecosystem.

Encounters may involve combat, stealth, or avoidance depending on the situation and available resources.

### Discovery

Players uncover narrative fragments that reveal the history of Blackwater Cove, the research conducted by scientific organizations, and the nature of the Rot ecosystem.

### Progression

As players learn more about the environment, they unlock new locations and advance deeper into Rot affected regions.

The loop then repeats as players explore new areas.

---

## 6. Player Journey

The player journey describes how the experience evolves from the beginning to the end of the game.

### Early Game

Players begin with limited knowledge about the Rot and the events that occurred in Blackwater Cove.

Early gameplay focuses on exploration, environmental observation, and basic survival.

Creatures appear less frequently and environments show early stages of Rot contamination.

---

### Mid Game

Players gain greater understanding of the Rot ecosystem and the research conducted by scientific organizations.

Encounters with mutated organisms become more frequent and environments show heavier biological transformation.

The player begins exploring research facilities and containment sites.

---

### Late Game

The player reaches areas where the Rot ecosystem has become fully established.

These environments are heavily transformed and contain the most dangerous organisms.

Narrative discoveries reveal the consequences of past scientific experiments and containment failures.

The player gains a deeper understanding of the Rot while realizing that its full nature remains uncertain.

---

## 7. Key Gameplay Systems

ROT relies on several core gameplay systems that work together to support the survival horror experience.

Major systems include:

- player movement and traversal
- combat mechanics
- stealth mechanics
- inventory and resource management
- environmental interaction
- Rot exposure mechanics
- enemy behavior and ecosystem interactions

Each system contributes to the overall experience of exploration, survival, and discovery.

---

## 8. Narrative Approach

The narrative in ROT is primarily delivered through environmental storytelling and fragmented discoveries.

Players uncover information through:

- abandoned research facilities
- scientific logs and notes
- personal belongings left behind during evacuation
- environmental evidence of Rot transformation

The story unfolds gradually as players piece together events through observation and exploration.
