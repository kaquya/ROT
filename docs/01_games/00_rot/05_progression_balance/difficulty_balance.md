# 1. Overview

This document defines how difficulty is balanced throughout ROT.

Difficulty in ROT is not defined purely by enemy strength. Instead, it emerges from the interaction of several systems including enemy encounters, resource scarcity, environmental hazards, and player decision making.

The goal is to create a consistent survival horror experience where the player feels vulnerable but capable of surviving through careful play.

Difficulty balancing focuses on three main areas:

- difficulty scaling across the game
- resource scarcity management
- tuning player challenge

---

# 2. Difficulty Design Philosophy

Difficulty in ROT is designed around tension rather than punishment.

Players should feel pressure and uncertainty, but they should rarely feel that failure was unavoidable.

Key principles include:

- tension over frustration  
- fair encounters with clear feedback  
- meaningful consequences for mistakes  
- consistent challenge escalation

The game should reward careful exploration and tactical thinking rather than fast reflexes alone.

---

# 3. Difficulty Scaling

Difficulty gradually increases as the player progresses through Blackwater Cove.

Scaling occurs through several methods.

## Enemy Strength

Enemies become more dangerous in later districts through:

- higher health values  
- increased damage output  
- improved detection capabilities  

Enemy variants may also appear in later areas.

---

## Encounter Complexity

Encounters become more complex as the game progresses.

Examples include:

- multiple enemy types appearing together  
- tighter combat environments  
- ambush encounters  
- environmental hazards during combat

Later encounters require more tactical thinking from the player.

---

## Environmental Threats

The environment itself becomes more dangerous as Rot contamination spreads.

Examples include:

- toxic Rot biomass areas  
- biological hazards  
- unstable structures  
- limited visibility environments

Environmental danger reinforces the sense that the ecosystem is evolving.

---

# 4. Resource Scarcity

Resource scarcity is a central element of survival horror gameplay.

Players should never feel fully equipped for every encounter.

## Limited Supplies

Items such as ammunition, medical supplies, and crafting materials should be carefully distributed across the world.

Players must decide when to use resources and when to conserve them.

---

## Exploration Rewards

Thorough exploration may reward players with additional supplies.

Hidden rooms, optional areas, and puzzle rewards may contain valuable resources.

This encourages players to explore the environment carefully.

---

## Risk vs Reward

Some resource locations should require the player to take risks.

Examples include:

- entering heavily contaminated areas  
- confronting enemy groups  
- solving puzzles in dangerous environments

Players must decide whether the reward is worth the risk.

---

# 5. Player Challenge Tuning

Player challenge is controlled through several balancing mechanisms.

---

## Damage Balance

Enemy damage values must feel threatening but not unfair.

Players should usually survive multiple hits unless facing powerful enemies.

---

## Healing Availability

Healing items should remain limited but not extremely rare.

Players should feel pressure when injured but still have opportunities to recover.

---

## Stamina Pressure

Stamina limits player movement and combat endurance.

Poor stamina management should create vulnerability during encounters.

---

## Rot Exposure Pressure

The Rot Exposure System creates long term environmental pressure.

Players must balance exploration speed with contamination risk.

---

# 6. Difficulty Modes (Optional)

If multiple difficulty modes are included, adjustments may affect:

- enemy health values  
- resource availability  
- Rot exposure speed  
- damage values

These adjustments should preserve the core survival horror experience.

Difficulty modes should not drastically change core mechanics.

---

# 7. Testing and Iteration

Difficulty balance must be refined through playtesting.

Testing should evaluate:

- encounter fairness  
- resource availability  
- player survival rates  
- pacing of tension and relief

Feedback from testing sessions is critical to maintaining a balanced experience.

---

# 8. Integration with Other Systems

Difficulty balancing interacts with several gameplay systems.

Examples include:

- Enemy Stats for combat difficulty  
- Player Stats for survivability  
- Encounter Design for combat pacing  
- Inventory and Crafting Systems for resource availability

Balancing changes should always consider the impact on these connected systems.

---

# 9. Purpose of This Document

This document defines how difficulty is structured and balanced within ROT.

By carefully controlling enemy strength, resource distribution, and environmental hazards, the game maintains a tense survival horror experience while remaining fair and engaging.
