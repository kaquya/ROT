## 1. Overview

Gameplay pillars define the fundamental principles that guide the design of ROT.  
Every gameplay system, environment, enemy encounter, and narrative element should support these pillars.

The pillars represent the core experience that the game intends to deliver to the player.  
If a mechanic, system, or feature does not reinforce these pillars, it should be reconsidered or removed.

ROT is built around four primary gameplay pillars:

- survival horror tension  
- ecological horror  
- exploration  
- narrative discovery  

Together, these pillars create a game that focuses on atmosphere, investigation, and survival within a transforming ecosystem.

---

## 2. Survival Horror Tension

### Design Goal

Players should feel vulnerable in a dangerous environment where resources are limited and threats cannot always be confronted directly.

Survival tension is created by uncertainty, limited supplies, and unpredictable encounters with Rot organisms.

### Key Elements

Survival horror tension is supported through:

- limited ammunition and healing items  
- dangerous enemy encounters  
- environmental hazards  
- restricted visibility and lighting conditions  
- unpredictable creature behavior  

Combat should never feel effortless. Encounters should require careful planning and awareness of the environment.

### Player Experience

Players should constantly weigh risks and decisions such as:

- whether to engage or avoid enemies  
- when to use limited resources  
- how to navigate unfamiliar environments safely  

The result should be a continuous sense of tension and caution.

---

## 3. Ecological Horror

### Design Goal

Horror in ROT emerges from biological transformation and ecological restructuring.

The Rot is not simply an infection. It reorganizes living systems and environments.

The player explores ecosystems where familiar organisms and environments have been transformed into unfamiliar forms.

### Key Elements

Ecological horror is reinforced through:

- mutated wildlife and hybrid organisms  
- environments overtaken by biological growth  
- ecosystems with new predator and prey relationships  
- organic structures emerging from human architecture  

Creatures and environments should appear unsettling because they follow a biological logic that is unfamiliar to the player.

### Player Experience

Players gradually realize that the Rot is not chaotic destruction.  
It is a system creating a new ecological order.

This realization creates a unique form of horror based on transformation rather than simple threat.

---

## 4. Exploration

### Design Goal

Exploration is a central component of ROT gameplay.

Players should feel motivated to investigate environments in order to uncover resources, narrative clues, and new paths.

The world should reward curiosity and observation.

### Key Elements

Exploration is supported through:

- interconnected environments  
- hidden paths and optional areas  
- environmental puzzles and obstacles  
- gradual unlocking of new regions  

The level design should encourage players to revisit previously explored areas after gaining new knowledge or access.

### Player Experience

Players should feel like investigators navigating an abandoned region.

Careful exploration reveals secrets, resources, and narrative fragments that deepen the player's understanding of the world.

---

## 5. Narrative Discovery

### Design Goal

The story of ROT should unfold gradually through discovery rather than direct exposition.

Players learn about the Rot, the history of Blackwater Cove, and the actions of research organizations through environmental clues.

### Key Elements

Narrative discovery is delivered through:

- environmental storytelling scenes  
- research logs and scientific records  
- abandoned laboratories and containment sites  
- personal items and written notes left behind by former residents  

Information is fragmented across locations and must be assembled by the player.

### Player Experience

Players piece together the history of the outbreak and the scientific investigation surrounding the Rot.

Understanding the narrative becomes part of the exploration process.

---

## 6. Interaction Between Pillars

The gameplay pillars reinforce each other.

Exploration leads players into dangerous environments that create survival tension.

Ecological horror shapes the creatures and environments encountered during exploration.

Narrative discovery provides context for the ecosystems and events observed by the player.

This interconnected structure ensures that each pillar strengthens the overall experience.

---

## 7. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the core gameplay pillars of ROT.

All gameplay systems, level design decisions, and narrative elements should support these pillars.

Maintaining focus on these principles ensures that the final game experience remains consistent with the intended survival horror identity of the project.
