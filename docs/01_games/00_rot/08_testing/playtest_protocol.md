# 1. Overview

This document defines the playtest protocol used during the development of ROT.

Playtesting is used to observe real player behavior in order to evaluate gameplay clarity, tension, pacing, and overall player experience.

Unlike internal system testing, playtesting focuses on how players naturally interact with the game rather than verifying technical correctness.

The goal of playtesting is to identify:

- confusing mechanics
- difficulty spikes
- unclear objectives
- pacing issues
- unintended player behavior
- navigation problems

Playtesting helps ensure that the intended survival horror experience is communicated effectively.

---

# 2. Playtest Objectives

Each playtest session should focus on specific objectives.

Common objectives include:

- evaluating exploration flow
- measuring tension and pacing
- observing combat behavior
- assessing puzzle clarity
- testing navigation through environments

Playtest sessions should not attempt to evaluate every aspect of the game at once.

Focused testing produces clearer results.

---

# 3. Playtester Selection

Playtesters should ideally represent players who are unfamiliar with the game.

External testers are especially valuable because they do not possess internal development knowledge.

Types of testers may include:

- internal developers not involved in the tested system
- external testers
- players experienced with survival horror games
- players unfamiliar with the genre

Different testers may reveal different types of issues.

---

# 4. Playtest Environment

Playtests should occur in a controlled environment that allows observation and data collection.

Requirements include:

- a stable game build
- minimal external distractions
- screen recording when possible
- note-taking by observers

Observers should avoid interfering with the player during gameplay.

---

# 5. Observer Responsibilities

Observers monitor the player during the session and record important observations.

Observers should focus on:

- player confusion
- hesitation during exploration
- unexpected behavior
- difficulty spikes
- navigation problems

Observers should not guide the player unless the player becomes completely stuck.

Natural player behavior provides the most valuable feedback.

---

# 6. Player Instructions

Before the playtest begins, the player should receive simple instructions.

Typical instructions include:

- play naturally
- speak thoughts aloud if comfortable
- attempt to progress through the environment
- do not worry about making mistakes

Players should not be given detailed explanations about mechanics.

Discovering mechanics naturally helps evaluate design clarity.

---

# 7. Observation Focus Areas

Observers should monitor several aspects of the player experience.

## Navigation

Observe how easily players understand the layout of environments.

Indicators of problems include:

- repeated backtracking
- confusion about where to go
- failure to notice important landmarks

---

## Combat Behavior

Observe how players react during enemy encounters.

Examples include:

- hesitation during combat
- misunderstanding attack patterns
- difficulty managing resources

These observations help balance combat difficulty.

---

## Puzzle Interaction

Observe how players approach puzzles.

Indicators of puzzle problems include:

- confusion about puzzle mechanics
- excessive trial and error
- inability to understand puzzle goals

Puzzle solutions should feel logical rather than random.

---

## Resource Management

Observe how players use resources.

Examples include:

- hoarding items
- running out of critical supplies
- misunderstanding item usefulness

Resource usage helps evaluate survival mechanics.

---

# 8. Recording Observations

Observers should record observations throughout the playtest session.

Important notes include:

- player actions
- moments of confusion
- emotional reactions
- gameplay flow issues

Whenever possible, record timestamps linked to gameplay events.

---

# 9. Post-Playtest Interview

After the session, the player should be asked questions about their experience.

Example questions include:

- What parts of the game felt confusing?
- Did you ever feel lost while exploring?
- Were enemy encounters fair or frustrating?
- Which moments felt the most tense?
- Which moments felt slow or unclear?

Player feedback provides valuable insight into their experience.

---

# 10. Playtest Data Analysis

After the session, observers should analyze collected data.

Focus areas include:

- repeated player mistakes
- navigation confusion
- pacing problems
- unclear mechanics

If multiple testers encounter the same issue, it likely indicates a design problem.

---

# 11. Iteration

Playtest results should lead to design adjustments.

Possible adjustments include:

- improving environmental guidance
- rebalancing enemy encounters
- clarifying puzzle mechanics
- adjusting resource availability

Playtesting should be repeated after major design changes.

---

# 12. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the protocol used for conducting playtests in ROT.

By observing real player behavior and analyzing their experiences, the development team can refine gameplay systems and ensure that the intended survival horror experience is communicated clearly and effectively.
