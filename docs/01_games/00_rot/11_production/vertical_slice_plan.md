# Vertical Slice Plan

Project: ROT  
Version: 1.1  
Document Type: Production  
Category: Development Milestone  
Scope: Vertical Slice  
Status: Core Reference

---

# 1. Overview

This document defines the vertical slice milestone for ROT.

A vertical slice is a small but complete portion of the game that demonstrates the intended gameplay experience, technical architecture, and artistic direction.

The goal of the vertical slice is not to build a large amount of content. Instead it proves that the core systems of the game function together in a cohesive way.

The vertical slice should represent the final gameplay quality and tone of the game on a smaller scale.

---

# 2. Purpose of the Vertical Slice

The vertical slice serves several important purposes.

It allows the development team to validate the following elements:

- core gameplay loop
- combat and stealth mechanics
- Rot exposure mechanics
- enemy behavior
- level design structure
- narrative discovery
- visual identity
- audio design

If the vertical slice works well, the team can confidently scale development to the full game.

---

# 3. Vertical Slice Location

The vertical slice will take place in a section of the **Residential District** and the **Downtown District** of Blackwater Cove.

These areas are selected because they represent the earliest stages of the outbreak and introduce the player to the core gameplay systems.

The slice should include:

- several residential streets
- a small interior environment
- a connection route into downtown
- at least one safe zone
- early Rot contamination areas

This provides enough environmental variety to test exploration and encounter design.

---

# 4. Vertical Slice Gameplay Goals

The vertical slice must demonstrate the full gameplay loop of ROT.

The player should be able to:

explore → scavenge → survive → uncover narrative → progress

Key mechanics that must be functional include:

- player movement
- combat mechanics
- stealth system
- inventory system
- interaction system
- Rot exposure system
- enemy AI behavior
- UI systems

These systems should function together without requiring developer shortcuts.

---

# 5. Player Systems Required

The following player systems must be implemented for the vertical slice.

Movement systems

- walking
- sprinting
- crouching
- basic traversal

Player survival systems

- health
- stamina
- Rot exposure

Interaction systems

- opening doors
- collecting items
- activating devices

---

# 6. Enemy Systems Required

The vertical slice must include at least two enemy types.

Example enemy types:

Rotter  
Basic infected humanoid creature that patrols environments.

Collapsed Rotter  
A more aggressive variant that ambushes the player in confined areas.

Enemy behavior must demonstrate:

- patrol behavior
- detection through sound or vision
- chase behavior
- attack mechanics

---

# 7. Level Design Scope

The vertical slice level should contain a compact but interconnected environment.

The level should include:

- outdoor exploration spaces
- at least one interior building
- narrow traversal routes
- optional side areas

The layout should demonstrate the exploration philosophy used throughout the game.

This includes:

- branching paths
- environmental storytelling
- shortcut connections

---

# 8. Puzzle Integration

The vertical slice should include at least one puzzle interaction.

Example puzzle types:

- restoring power to unlock a door
- activating a control panel
- retrieving a key item to access a new area

The puzzle should demonstrate how environmental interactions are integrated with exploration.

---

# 9. Narrative Content

The vertical slice should include early narrative discovery elements.

Examples include:

- one or two Halcyon records
- environmental storytelling scenes
- traces of evacuation attempts

Narrative content should hint at the larger mystery of the Rot without fully explaining it.

---

# 10. Rot System Demonstration

The vertical slice must demonstrate the Rot exposure mechanic.

This includes:

- contaminated environments
- visual Rot growth in the environment
- gradual contamination buildup for the player

Players should learn that prolonged exposure to corrupted areas is dangerous.

---

# 11. Safe Zone Implementation

The vertical slice should contain at least one safe zone.

Example location:

Elena's childhood home.

The safe zone should allow the player to:

- save progress
- manage inventory
- review collected documents

Safe zones should feel calm and secure compared to the surrounding environment.

---

# 12. Audio and Atmosphere

The vertical slice must demonstrate the intended audio design of ROT.

This includes:

- ambient environmental sound
- distant creature noises
- tension building audio cues

Audio plays a major role in maintaining survival horror atmosphere.

---

# 13. Visual Identity

The vertical slice must represent the intended visual style of the game.

This includes:

- lighting tone
- environmental decay
- Rot visual growth patterns
- color palette consistency

Art quality should reflect the final visual direction of the project.

---

# 14. Success Criteria

The vertical slice is considered successful if the following conditions are met.

The player can complete a short gameplay segment that includes:

- exploration
- at least one enemy encounter
- puzzle interaction
- narrative discovery

All core gameplay systems function together without breaking immersion.

The slice should feel like a small but complete portion of the final game.

---

# 15. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the scope and requirements for the ROT vertical slice milestone.

Completing this milestone proves that the game's systems, mechanics, and narrative structure work together as intended before full production begins.
