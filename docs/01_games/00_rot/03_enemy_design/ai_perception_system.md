# 1. Overview

The AI Perception System defines how enemies detect and interpret the presence of the player within the environment.

Enemy perception in ROT is based on sensory systems that simulate how creatures observe their surroundings. Detection does not occur instantly. Instead, enemies interpret signals such as sound, movement, and visual presence before confirming the player as a threat.

The system is designed to support stealth gameplay while maintaining tension. Players who move carefully and remain aware of their surroundings can avoid detection, while careless actions may quickly alert nearby enemies.

The perception system primarily relies on three components:

- sound detection  
- vision detection  
- investigation behavior  

These components determine how enemies transition between passive behavior and active engagement.

---

# 2. Perception Design Philosophy

Enemy perception follows several guiding principles.

### Gradual Awareness

Enemies should not instantly detect the player. Instead, detection builds gradually as enemies receive sensory information.

This allows players time to react and adjust their behavior.

---

### Sensory Interpretation

Enemies interpret environmental signals rather than simply reacting to the player's presence.

Examples include:

- hearing unusual noise  
- noticing movement within their vision range  
- observing disturbances in the environment  

This approach creates more believable enemy behavior.

---

### Player Feedback

Players should be able to understand when enemies are becoming suspicious.

Visual cues, animations, or sound responses from enemies should communicate their awareness level.

This feedback helps players react before full detection occurs.

---

# 3. Sound Detection

Sound detection allows enemies to react to noise generated by the player or the environment.

Many creatures rely heavily on sound to identify potential threats.

### Sound Sources

Common sound sources include:

- sprinting or rapid movement  
- weapon discharge  
- object interaction  
- environmental disturbances  

Different actions produce different noise intensities.

---

### Sound Radius

Each sound event generates a detection radius.

Enemies within this radius may respond by turning toward the source or investigating the location.

Louder sounds create larger detection areas.

---

### Sound Interpretation

When a sound is detected, enemies do not immediately attack.

Instead they may:

- turn toward the direction of the sound  
- move cautiously toward the location  
- search the surrounding area  

This process increases tension and allows players to recover if they react quickly.

---

# 4. Vision Detection

Vision detection determines how enemies visually identify the player.

Enemies detect the player when the player enters their field of view and remains visible long enough to confirm the presence of a target.

### Field of View

Each enemy has a defined visual cone representing their field of view.

Players within this cone may be detected depending on several factors.

---

### Detection Factors

Visual detection depends on multiple conditions.

Examples include:

- distance between enemy and player  
- player movement speed  
- player posture (standing or crouching)  
- environmental lighting conditions  

Players moving quickly in well lit areas are easier to detect.

---

### Detection Time

Visual detection may require a short confirmation period.

If the player briefly crosses an enemy's field of view, the enemy may become suspicious but not immediately enter combat.

This system prevents instant detection during momentary exposure.

---

# 5. Investigation Behavior

Investigation behavior occurs when enemies detect unusual stimuli but have not yet confirmed the player's presence.

Examples of triggers include:

- hearing a suspicious sound  
- briefly seeing movement  
- noticing environmental disturbances  

During investigation:

- enemies move toward the suspicious location  
- enemies search the area  
- enemies remain alert for further signals  

If no additional evidence appears, enemies eventually return to their normal behavior state.

---

# 6. Awareness Levels

Enemy awareness typically progresses through several stages.

### Passive

Enemy is unaware of the player and continues normal behavior such as patrolling or idle activity.

---

### Suspicious

Enemy has detected unusual stimuli and begins investigating the area.

---

### Alert

Enemy has confirmed the presence of the player and begins pursuit or combat behavior.

---

### Search

If the enemy loses track of the player, it may search nearby areas before returning to passive behavior.

---

# 7. Environmental Influence

Environmental conditions can influence enemy perception.

Examples include:

- darkness reducing visual detection range  
- loud environmental noise masking player movement  
- dense Rot growth obstructing visibility  

Level design can therefore influence stealth strategies and encounter outcomes.

---

# 8. Integration with Other Systems

The AI Perception System interacts with several gameplay systems.

Examples include:

- the Stealth System for player movement and visibility mechanics  
- the Audio Gameplay System for sound generation and detection  
- the Enemy AI System for behavior state transitions  
- the Level Design System for lighting and environmental cover  

These interactions ensure that perception remains consistent across gameplay systems.

# 9. Purpose of This Document

This document defines how enemies detect and interpret player presence in ROT.

By combining sound detection, vision detection, and investigation behavior, the AI Perception System creates believable and tense encounters that reward careful movement and environmental awareness.
