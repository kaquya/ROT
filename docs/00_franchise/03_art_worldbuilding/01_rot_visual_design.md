# ROT – Rot Visual Design

Version: 1.0  
Status: Biological Design Reference

---

# 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the visual and biological design language of **the Rot ecosystem**.

The Rot is the defining visual element of the ROT franchise.  
Its appearance must remain consistent across all games.

This document establishes:

• Rot biological structure  
• visual mutation rules  
• environmental growth patterns  
• color palette and materials  
• organism formation logic  

All Rot-related visuals must follow these rules.

---

# 2. Rot Biological Identity

The Rot is not a fungus, parasite, or virus.

Instead, it behaves like a **planetary biological infrastructure** capable of recording and reconstructing life.

Visually, the Rot should appear like a **hybrid between several biological systems**.

Primary inspirations include:

• vascular tissue networks  
• marine invertebrates  
• fungal growth systems  
• parasitic organisms  
• cellular biological structures  

The Rot should feel:

• alien  
• organic  
• ancient  
• adaptive  

However, it should never appear supernatural or magical.

---

# 3. Core Rot Visual Characteristics

All Rot structures share several common visual traits.

---

## 3.1 Vein Networks

One of the most recognizable Rot structures is the **vascular network**.

These networks appear as branching tissue lines spreading across surfaces.

Characteristics include:

• branching organic pathways  
• pulsing biological movement  
• semi-translucent tissue layers  
• organic connections between distant structures  

These networks represent the Rot’s internal transport system.

---

## 3.2 Organic Tissue Layers

Rot growth often resembles layers of exposed biological tissue.

Common characteristics:

• fibrous connective structures  
• stretched membrane surfaces  
• organic folds and wrinkles  
• exposed vascular veins  

These layers often cover environmental surfaces.

---

## 3.3 Growth Nodes

Growth nodes are bulb-like structures embedded within Rot ecosystems.

These nodes may resemble:

• organic organs  
• pulsating sacs  
• clustered biological masses  

Growth nodes often serve as:

• resource distribution centers  
• organism spawning locations  
• biological communication hubs  

In gameplay, these structures often act as environmental weak points.

---

## 3.4 Tendril Structures

Tendrils are long organic extensions used by the Rot ecosystem to expand.

They may appear as:

• rope-like biological cables  
• muscular organic strands  
• branching tissue structures

Tendrils frequently connect different parts of the ecosystem.

---

# 4. Rot Growth Behavior

Rot growth should follow **logical environmental patterns** rather than appearing randomly placed.

Growth should look like a living organism spreading through its environment.

---

## 4.1 Structural Attachment

Rot growth often anchors itself to surfaces.

Common attachment points include:

• walls  
• ceilings  
• floors  
• structural beams  
• pipes or cables  

The Rot often spreads along existing structural pathways.

---

## 4.2 Environmental Penetration

The Rot may break through surfaces while spreading.

Examples include:

• emerging through cracked concrete  
• splitting wooden floors  
• pushing through walls  
• growing along metal structures  

These interactions reinforce the impression that the Rot is physically expanding.

---

## 4.3 Surface Integration

Instead of sitting on top of surfaces, Rot growth should appear embedded into them.

Examples:

• walls partially fused with organic tissue  
• floors merging with biological structures  
• machinery partially consumed by Rot growth

Integration makes environments appear biologically altered rather than decorated.

---

# 5. Rot Color Palette

Color is an important part of Rot identity.

The Rot color palette must remain consistent across the franchise.

---

## 5.1 Primary Tissue Colors

The most common Rot colors resemble biological tissue.

Examples include:

• deep crimson red  
• dark maroon  
• organic purple tones  
• pale flesh colors

These tones resemble exposed biological structures.

---

## 5.2 Secondary Growth Colors

Certain Rot structures may display additional coloration.

Examples include:

• pale fungal white  
• sickly yellow fluids  
• greenish organic growths

These colors help distinguish different biological structures.

---

## 5.3 Fluid and Organic Highlights

Rot ecosystems often contain organic fluid.

Examples include:

• dripping biological residue  
• pulsing organic fluid sacs  
• wet tissue reflections

Surface materials should appear moist or organic.

---

# 6. Rot Surface Materials

Rot surfaces should emphasize biological realism.

Common visual characteristics include:

• wet reflections  
• fibrous tissue strands  
• semi-translucent membrane surfaces  
• soft organic lighting

Materials should avoid sharp metallic textures.

Everything should appear soft, biological, and alive.

---

# 7. Environmental Rot Systems

Rot ecosystems transform entire environments.

Several environmental systems appear frequently.

---

## 7.1 Vein Networks

Large vascular networks may spread across entire environments.

These structures often connect multiple growth clusters.

They function as the circulatory system of the Rot ecosystem.

---

## 7.2 Rot Bloom Structures

Rot Blooms are large biological masses embedded within environments.

Characteristics include:

• massive organic structures  
• multiple growth nodes  
• spawning of smaller organisms

Blooms often serve as major environmental bosses.

---

## 7.3 Spore Fields

Certain areas may contain airborne spores.

Visual elements include:

• floating organic particles  
• drifting biological mist  
• glowing microscopic structures

These areas may indicate heavy Rot contamination.

---

# 8. Rot Creature Formation

Creatures created by the Rot are not traditional infected hosts.

Instead, they represent **biological reconstruction attempts** produced by the Rot ecosystem.

When the Rot absorbs biological material, it stores structural patterns from the organism.

Later, the system may reconstruct new organisms using fragments of these patterns.

Because the archive may contain incomplete or mismatched data, the resulting creatures often appear distorted.

---

## 8.1 Reconstruction Imperfection

Rot organisms rarely appear anatomically correct.

Common reconstruction errors include:

• fused skeletal structures  
• misaligned limbs  
• duplicated anatomical features  
• exposed internal tissue  

These imperfections reinforce the unsettling nature of the Rot ecosystem.

---

## 8.2 Hybridization

In environments with multiple biological sources, the Rot may combine fragments of different organisms.

Examples include:

• human and animal anatomical features merging  
• skeletal structures incorporating multiple species  
• limbs forming from mixed biological patterns  

Hybridization increases the visual variety of Rot creatures.

---

## 8.3 Retained Instincts

Some reconstructed organisms retain fragments of behavioral memory.

For example:

• predators may retain hunting instincts  
• humans may retain aggressive reactions  
• animals may retain territorial behavior

These instincts influence enemy behavior in gameplay.

---

# 9. Rot Ecosystem Hierarchy

The Rot ecosystem appears to contain several layers of biological organization.

These layers help maintain the stability of the ecosystem.

---

## 9.1 Growth Structures

Growth structures are stationary biological formations embedded in the environment.

Examples include:

• growth nodes  
• vein networks  
• Rot Blooms  
• spore sacs

These structures function as the infrastructure of the Rot ecosystem.

---

## 9.2 Scavenger Organisms

Some creatures act as recyclers within the ecosystem.

Their purpose is to break down organic matter and feed it back into the Rot network.

These organisms typically appear in heavily infected areas.

---

## 9.3 Predator Organisms

Certain creatures appear specialized for hunting.

These organisms eliminate threats to the ecosystem and maintain biological balance.

Examples include Stalker-type creatures.

---

## 9.4 Apex Organisms

Large creatures may form when the Rot ecosystem reaches advanced development stages.

These organisms often act as territorial guardians of important growth structures.

Apex organisms typically serve as boss encounters.

---

# 10. Rot Environmental Transformation

As the Rot ecosystem spreads, environments gradually change.

These changes occur in recognizable stages.

---

## 10.1 Early Contamination

Initial infection signs are subtle.

Examples include:

• small tissue growth patches  
• organic residue on surfaces  
• unusual biological fluids  

The environment still appears largely intact.

---

## 10.2 Structural Integration

Rot growth begins merging with architecture.

Examples include:

• vein networks spreading through walls  
• biological tissue covering surfaces  
• machinery partially consumed by organic material

Environmental structures begin to feel biologically altered.

---

## 10.3 Ecosystem Formation

Large Rot structures appear.

Examples include:

• Rot Blooms  
• tissue bridges connecting structures  
• large growth clusters

These environments begin to resemble living ecosystems.

---

## 10.4 Biological Domination

At the most advanced stage, the Rot ecosystem dominates the environment.

Examples include:

• massive organic chambers  
• environments resembling internal biological organs  
• large-scale biological networks

Human architecture becomes secondary to Rot biology.

---

# 11. Rot Megastructures

In advanced outbreaks, the Rot may produce massive biological formations.

These structures serve as major ecosystem hubs.

---

## 11.1 Convergence Nodes

Convergence nodes appear to act as central junction points in the Rot network.

Characteristics include:

• massive biological chambers  
• complex vascular networks  
• strong organism activity

These structures may serve as the core of an outbreak zone.

---

## 11.2 Bloom Guardians

Some Rot Blooms develop protective organisms.

These creatures act as defenders of the ecosystem.

Bloom Guardians often serve as major boss encounters.

---

## 11.3 Biological Caverns

In advanced infections, entire environments may become biological caverns.

Characteristics include:

• walls resembling living tissue  
• pulsing environmental surfaces  
• organic lighting from Rot structures

These locations represent the most extreme stage of Rot domination.

---

# 12. Visual Consistency Rules

To maintain consistent Rot design across the franchise, several rules must always be followed.

---

## 12.1 The Rot Is Always Biological

Rot structures must resemble living tissue.

They should never resemble machinery or technological devices.

---

## 12.2 Growth Has Direction

Rot growth should follow believable environmental pathways.

Random placement of growth structures should be avoided.

---

## 12.3 Imperfection Is Essential

Rot organisms should never appear perfectly symmetrical or anatomically correct.

Biological reconstruction errors are part of the design language.

---

## 12.4 Environments Should Feel Alive

Rot environments should feel like living ecosystems rather than static decorations.

Subtle movement and organic structure help reinforce this impression.

---

# 13. Design Scope for Solo Development

Because ROT is a solo-developed project, Rot visual assets must remain manageable.

Several strategies help maintain visual variety without excessive asset creation.

---

## 13.1 Modular Rot Assets

Rot structures should be designed as modular components.

Examples include:

• vein segments  
• tissue patches  
• growth nodes  
• tendril clusters

These pieces can be combined to create complex growth patterns.

---

## 13.2 Texture Variations

A small number of high-quality textures can support many visual variations.

Examples include:

• tissue textures  
• membrane surfaces  
• vascular structures

Texture blending can create diverse environments.

---

## 13.3 Reusable Growth Systems

Rot growth systems should be reused across multiple environments.

Examples include:

• wall growth systems  
• floor growth systems  
• ceiling tendril clusters

This approach reduces modeling workload while maintaining visual consistency.
