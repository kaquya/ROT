## 1. Overview

This document defines the production philosophy used for projects within the ROT franchise.

The purpose of these principles is to guide how ROT games and related projects should be developed. They establish a structured approach to development that prioritizes clarity, iteration, and consistency with the creative identity of the franchise.

ROT projects should follow a development approach that emphasizes strong documentation, clear system design, and gradual expansion of complexity.

These principles ensure that the franchise can grow sustainably while maintaining quality and creative coherence.

---

## 2. Production Philosophy

ROT projects should follow a development philosophy based on three key ideas.

### Clarity of Design

Every system, environment, and narrative element should be clearly defined before large scale implementation begins.

Design documents should describe the purpose of each system and how it interacts with other systems.

Clear documentation helps reduce production confusion and ensures that team members share a consistent understanding of the project.

---

### Iterative Development

Development should occur in stages that gradually increase complexity.

Early versions of systems should focus on core functionality. Additional features and polish should be added after the foundation has been validated.

Iteration allows teams to test ideas, identify problems early, and improve design decisions before full production begins.

---

### Consistent Worldbuilding

All production decisions should remain aligned with the established rules of the ROT universe.

Visual design, creature design, environmental storytelling, and gameplay systems should follow the franchise documentation.

Consistency ensures that each project feels like part of the same universe.

---

## 3. Team Structure

ROT projects should maintain a small but clearly organized team structure.

Each team member should have a defined area of responsibility.

Typical roles may include:

- design leadership responsible for gameplay systems and mechanics  
- narrative leadership responsible for story and worldbuilding  
- environment artists responsible for level creation and environmental storytelling  
- creature artists responsible for Rot organism design  
- programmers responsible for gameplay systems and technical architecture  
- audio designers responsible for environmental sound and atmosphere  

Clear communication between these roles is essential to maintain a consistent project vision.

---

## 4. Development Phases

ROT projects should progress through several development phases.

Each phase focuses on specific production goals.

### Concept Phase

The concept phase establishes the foundation of the project.

Activities during this phase may include:

- defining the project vision  
- identifying the core gameplay pillars  
- outlining narrative direction  
- establishing visual identity for the specific project  

The goal of this phase is to determine whether the project concept aligns with the ROT franchise.

---

### Preproduction Phase

Preproduction focuses on design planning and technical preparation.

Common tasks include:

- creating detailed design documentation  
- defining gameplay systems  
- planning environments and levels  
- establishing technical architecture  

At the end of preproduction, the team should have a clear plan for development.

---

### Vertical Slice Phase

The vertical slice is a small but complete section of the game that demonstrates how the final experience will function.

This slice should include:

- core gameplay systems  
- representative environments  
- enemy encounters  
- narrative elements  

The vertical slice validates that the design and production pipeline function correctly.

---

### Production Phase

The production phase focuses on building the full game.

During this phase, the team expands upon the systems and workflows validated during the vertical slice.

Content creation, level design, creature design, and system implementation occur at scale.

---

### Polishing Phase

The polishing phase focuses on refinement.

Activities include:

- gameplay balancing  
- performance optimization  
- bug fixing  
- visual and audio improvements  

The goal is to ensure that the final experience meets the quality standards of the ROT franchise.

---

## 5. Documentation Standards

Documentation plays a central role in ROT production.

Design decisions, systems, and worldbuilding rules should be documented clearly and updated as development progresses.

Important documentation categories include:

- gameplay systems  
- level design  
- creature design  
- worldbuilding and lore  
- technical architecture  

Maintaining accurate documentation allows the project to remain organized even as complexity increases.

---

## 6. Communication Principles

Effective communication is essential for successful production.

Team members should regularly share updates regarding their work, challenges, and progress.

Key communication practices include:

- regular development meetings  
- clear task tracking  
- documentation updates when systems change  
- early discussion of potential technical or design challenges  

Open communication helps prevent misunderstandings and production delays.

---

## 7. Scope Management

ROT projects should avoid excessive scope expansion.

Features should be added only if they support the core gameplay experience.

If new ideas arise during development, they should be evaluated carefully before being integrated into the project.

Maintaining a controlled scope ensures that the project remains achievable within its production timeline.

---

## 8. Quality Standards

Every ROT project should meet consistent quality standards.

These standards apply to:

- gameplay mechanics  
- visual presentation  
- narrative delivery  
- technical stability  

Quality should be prioritized over quantity of features.

A smaller, polished experience is preferable to a larger but unfinished project.

---

## 9. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the production principles used to guide ROT franchise development.

By following these principles, teams can maintain organized workflows, consistent creative direction, and sustainable production practices.

These guidelines ensure that ROT projects are developed efficiently while preserving the identity and quality of the franchise.
