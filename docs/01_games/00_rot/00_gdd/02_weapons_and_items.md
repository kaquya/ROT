# Weapons and Items

This document defines the **weapon categories, item ecosystem, and resource structure** used in ROT.

The goal is to create a **balanced survival economy** where resources are limited and every item has value.

Players must constantly decide:

- what to carry
- what to leave behind
- when to use limited resources

Weapons and items are designed to support **survival horror pacing rather than power fantasy**.

---

# Weapon Philosophy

Weapons in ROT are tools for survival rather than domination.

Key design principles:

• ammunition is limited  
• enemies are dangerous even when armed  
• combat is often avoidable  
• weapon variety supports different play styles  

Players are encouraged to balance **stealth, combat, and resource management**.

---

# Weapon Categories

Weapons are divided into several categories.

---

## Handguns

Handguns are the most common firearm in the game.

They provide:

- moderate damage
- reliable accuracy
- manageable recoil
- relatively common ammunition

Handguns are designed to be the player's **primary fallback weapon**.

Example weapons:

M9 Pistol  
Police Revolver  

Typical characteristics:

Damage: Low–Medium  
Fire Rate: Medium  
Magazine Size: Medium  

---

## Shotguns

Shotguns deal high damage at close range.

They are effective against:

- Rotter groups
- aggressive enemies
- large mutated creatures

However, shotgun ammunition is scarce.

Example weapons:

Pump Shotgun

Typical characteristics:

Damage: High  
Fire Rate: Slow  
Range: Short  

---

## Rifles

Rifles provide long-range precision.

They are effective for:

- eliminating distant enemies
- targeting weak points
- conserving ammunition through precise shots

Example weapons:

Hunting Rifle

Typical characteristics:

Damage: High  
Fire Rate: Slow  
Accuracy: Very High  

---

## Melee Weapons

Melee weapons allow the player to conserve ammunition.

However, melee combat is risky.

Melee attacks place the player within close proximity to enemies.

Example weapons:

Combat Knife  
Metal Pipe  
Fire Axe  

Typical characteristics:

Damage: Low–Medium  
Range: Very Short  
Durability: Medium  

Some melee weapons may degrade with repeated use.

---

# Ammunition Types

Each firearm requires a specific ammunition type.

Ammunition occupies inventory space.

---

## Handgun Ammo

Used by:

- M9 Pistol
- Revolvers

Most common ammunition type.

---

## Shotgun Shells

Used by:

- Pump Shotguns

Rare but extremely powerful.

---

## Rifle Rounds

Used by:

- Hunting Rifles

Very limited supply.

Encourages careful aiming.

---

# Healing Items

Healing items restore the player's health.

These items are intentionally limited.

---

## Bandage

Basic healing item.

Restores a small amount of health.

Most common healing item.

---

## Medical Kit

Advanced healing item.

Restores a large amount of health.

Rare.

---

## Antitoxin

Specialized treatment item.

Used to counter specific environmental hazards or infections.

Rare.

---

# Crafting Materials

Crafting materials allow players to create useful items.

Crafting does not eliminate scarcity.

It allows players to convert materials into items they need most.

---

## Alcohol

Used for:

- disinfectants
- chemical crafting

---

## Chemical Compound

Used for:

- medical injections
- specialized crafting recipes

---

## Scrap Metal

Used for:

- mechanical repairs
- crafting tools

---

## Cloth

Used for:

- bandages
- makeshift items

---

# Utility Items

Utility items assist with exploration and progression.

---

## Flashlight Batteries

Power the player's flashlight.

Essential in dark environments.

---

## Mechanical Parts

Used to repair machinery or activate devices.

Often required for puzzles.

---

## Fuse

Used in electrical puzzle systems.

Restores power to certain areas.

---

## Keycards

Used to access restricted areas.

Common in facilities such as hospitals or research centers.

---

# Key Items

Key items are required for story progression.

These items cannot be discarded.

Examples include:

- keys
- access cards
- puzzle artifacts
- mechanical components

Key items remain in the player's inventory until their associated puzzle or event is completed.

---

# Inventory Interaction

All items occupy inventory slots.

Item categories include:

- weapons
- ammunition
- healing items
- crafting materials
- utility items
- key items

Limited inventory space forces players to make strategic decisions about what to carry.

---

# Item Placement Philosophy

Items are distributed throughout the environment to reward exploration.

Players may find items in:

- abandoned homes
- police stations
- hospital storage rooms
- industrial buildings
- hidden containers

High-risk areas may contain more valuable resources.

Players who explore carefully will be better prepared for future encounters.

---

# Resource Economy

ROT uses a **controlled survival economy**.

Resources should feel:

- valuable
- limited
- worth searching for

Players should never feel completely safe from resource shortages.

Scarcity maintains tension throughout the game.
