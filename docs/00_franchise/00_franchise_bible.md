# 1. Franchise Overview

ROT is a narrative-driven survival horror franchise built around a biological anomaly known as **The Rot**.

The series explores outbreak zones where this phenomenon spreads through living organisms and environments, producing hostile ecosystems and mutated lifeforms.

Each entry in the franchise takes place in a different location affected by the Rot phenomenon, gradually expanding the player’s understanding of the system behind it.

The franchise blends:

- survival resource management
- exploration-driven gameplay
- environmental storytelling
- psychological tension
- grounded action combat

ROT is designed to exist between two established survival horror traditions:

- the **survival-action gameplay** of modern horror games
- the **atmospheric psychological tension** of classic horror experiences

However, the ROT franchise differentiates itself through a **biological and ecological horror foundation** rather than traditional zombie or supernatural narratives.

The Rot is not simply a pathogen or infection.

It represents a **planetary biological phenomenon capable of reconstructing ecosystems using archived biological information**.

---

# 2. Franchise Design Philosophy

The ROT franchise is built around several core design philosophies that guide all entries in the series.

---

## 2.1 Ecological Horror

The central horror of ROT does not come from supernatural forces or traditional monsters.

Instead, it comes from the unsettling idea that humanity has discovered a **biological system far older and larger than itself**.

The Rot represents an ecological mechanism capable of reorganizing life itself.

The destruction caused by the Rot is not intentional.

From the system’s perspective, it may simply be performing a natural function.

---

## 2.2 Grounded Survival

ROT avoids exaggerated action hero gameplay.

Protagonists are typically **ordinary individuals with specialized professions**, not military operatives.

Examples may include:

- divers
- researchers
- engineers
- rescue workers
- local residents

These characters must survive environments that were never designed for combat.

---

## 2.3 Environmental Discovery

Narrative information in ROT is rarely delivered through large cinematic sequences.

Instead, the story is primarily revealed through:

- exploration
- environmental clues
- abandoned research logs
- survivor messages
- physical evidence within the environment

Players reconstruct the history of each outbreak by observing the world around them.

---

## 2.4 Escalating Mystery

Each game reveals more information about the Rot phenomenon, but the full truth is not immediately revealed.

The narrative expands outward from localized disasters to a broader understanding of the system behind them.

The franchise gradually transitions from:

local outbreak mystery → global biological revelation.

---

# 3. The Rot Phenomenon

The Rot is the central biological force in the ROT universe.

It is not a virus, parasite, or fungal infection.

Instead, the Rot appears to be part of a **planetary-scale biological infrastructure capable of recording, preserving, and reconstructing life**.

---

## 3.1 Biological Archive System

Evidence suggests the Rot functions as a **biological archive of life on Earth**.

Instead of simply consuming living organisms, the Rot appears to record structural information about them.

This information may later be used to reconstruct biological structures.

However, these reconstructions are rarely perfect.

Because modern ecosystems differ from those originally stored in the archive, the resulting organisms often appear distorted or incomplete.

Examples include:

- humanoid creatures with merged skeletal structures
- animals with partially reconstructed anatomy
- hybrid organisms formed from multiple biological patterns

These creatures are not intentionally hostile.

They are biological reconstructions created from mismatched ecological memory.

---

## 3.2 Distributed Biological Network

The Rot does not behave like a single organism.

Instead, it operates as a **distributed network of microscopic biological structures** capable of exchanging information across living systems.

This network allows the Rot to:

- integrate biological material
- exchange structural information
- coordinate growth patterns

As the network expands, environments begin to resemble living ecosystems rather than human-built structures.

---

## 3.3 Ecological Reconstruction

The Rot appears to reorganize ecosystems through a multi-stage process.

Stage 1 – Contact  
Microscopic Rot structures attach to organic material.

Stage 2 – Integration  
Biological systems become integrated into the Rot network.

Stage 3 – Reconstruction  
Original organisms lose structural stability and are reorganized.

Stage 4 – Ecosystem Formation  
Large-scale biological structures appear and form interconnected networks.

These ecosystems may continue evolving long after initial infection.

---

## 3.4 Intelligence Debate

It remains unclear whether the Rot possesses true intelligence.

Some observed behaviors suggest a form of adaptive system logic.

Examples include:

- growth expanding toward biological resources
- coordinated responses to disturbances
- network structures resembling communication pathways

Some researchers theorize that the Rot may represent a **self-regulating planetary system designed to restore ecosystems following catastrophic events**.

If this theory is correct, the Rot may have played a role in rebuilding life after ancient extinction events.

---

# 4. Rot Infection Effects

The Rot affects living organisms in several ways depending on environmental conditions and biological compatibility.

---

## 4.1 Human Infection

Humans exposed to the Rot undergo progressive biological restructuring.

Typical stages include:

Exposure  
Initial neurological and physiological disturbances.

Cognitive Collapse  
Loss of identity and emotional regulation.

Physical Mutation  
Visible biological restructuring begins.

Complete Integration  
The host becomes a Rot organism.

Fragments of memory or instinct may remain.

---

## 4.2 Animal Infection

Animals integrated into the Rot ecosystem often retain more mobility and behavioral instincts.

Examples include:

- territorial predators
- scavenger organisms
- pack-based hunting creatures

This results in dangerous wildlife variants.

---

## 4.3 Environmental Integration

The Rot spreads into environments as well as organisms.

Affected locations often display:

- organic growth across architecture
- vein-like tissue structures
- pulsating biological masses
- interconnected growth networks

Buildings and infrastructure may become partially integrated into the Rot ecosystem.

Over time, infected locations begin to resemble living biological systems.

---

# 5. Rot Organism Types

Rot infections produce several categories of hostile organisms.

These creatures form the enemy ecosystem encountered throughout the series.

---

## Rotters

The most common infected organisms.

Characteristics:

- derived from human hosts
- aggressive behavior
- unstable physical structure
- limited intelligence

Rotters typically attack in groups.

---

## Stalkers

Predatory variants created when the Rot stabilizes movement and hunting instincts.

Characteristics:

- high mobility
- stealth behavior
- ambush tactics
- active tracking of prey

Stalkers create tension in exploration environments.

---

## Spliced Organisms

Large creatures formed when multiple biological sources merge together.

Characteristics:

- massive bodies
- extreme durability
- distorted anatomy
- powerful attacks

These organisms often serve as mini-boss encounters.

---

## Bloom Organisms

Large Rot growth structures formed when environmental infection reaches extreme levels.

Characteristics:

- massive biological formations
- control surrounding ecosystem
- produce additional organisms

Blooms frequently function as major boss encounters.

---
 
 # 6. Major Organizations

While the Rot itself represents a natural biological phenomenon, human organizations play a significant role in discovering, studying, and attempting to control it.

The most important of these organizations is the **Halcyon Initiative**.

---

## 6.1 The Halcyon Initiative

The Halcyon Initiative is a global research organization dedicated to studying unusual biological systems and environmental anomalies.

Publicly, the organization presents itself as a scientific institution focused on ecological preservation and biological research.

Privately, Halcyon conducts extensive investigations into anomalous biological structures discovered in extreme environments such as:

- deep ocean trenches
- remote cave systems
- ancient geological formations
- isolated ecosystems

The discovery of the Rot organism represents Halcyon’s most significant and controversial research project.

---

## 6.2 Halcyon Philosophy

The founding philosophy of the Halcyon Initiative is based on a controversial belief:

Humanity cannot repair the planet’s damaged ecosystems through technology alone.

Instead, Halcyon researchers believe that the key to planetary restoration lies in discovering biological systems capable of rebuilding ecosystems at a large scale.

When Halcyon researchers encountered the Rot organism, many believed they had discovered a natural system capable of restoring life after ecological collapse.

This belief led to increasingly ambitious research efforts.

---

## 6.3 Internal Divisions

As Halcyon’s research into the Rot progressed, internal disagreements began to emerge.

Two major factions developed within the organization.

**Reconstructionists**

This group believed the Rot represented a natural planetary recovery mechanism.

They argued that humanity should learn to cooperate with the Rot ecosystem rather than attempting to suppress it.

**Containment Advocates**

This faction believed the Rot was far too poorly understood to be studied outside strict containment.

They warned that the organism’s ability to reorganize living matter represented an unpredictable ecological threat.

These disagreements eventually created instability within the organization’s research programs.

---

## 6.4 Hidden Facilities

Halcyon operates numerous research facilities across the world.

Many of these installations are located in remote or isolated environments.

Some facilities operate openly as legitimate research centers.

Others function as restricted laboratories focused specifically on Rot-related research.

These facilities often become focal points of Rot outbreaks when containment fails.

---

# 7. Recurring Symbolism

The Rot frequently produces recurring biological patterns.

One of the most recognizable is the **spiral formation**.

Spiral structures appear in:

- environmental Rot growths
- mutated organisms
- biological tissue formations
- Halcyon research diagrams

The reason for this recurring structure remains unknown.

Some researchers believe the spiral pattern reflects the way biological information moves through the Rot network.

Within the franchise, the spiral gradually becomes a visual symbol associated with the Rot phenomenon.

---

# 8. Outbreak Zones

Each ROT game focuses on a specific **outbreak zone**.

An outbreak zone is a location where the Rot ecosystem has begun integrating with a human environment.

These locations vary widely in geography and scale.

Examples may include:

- coastal towns
- isolated research facilities
- abandoned cities
- forest regions
- underground cave networks

Every outbreak zone represents a different stage of Rot ecosystem development.

Early outbreaks may show minimal environmental transformation.

Later outbreaks may display large-scale biological integration with entire landscapes.

---

## 8.1 Environmental Transformation

As the Rot spreads through an outbreak zone, environments gradually change.

Typical progression includes:

**Initial Stage**

Small biological growths appear.

Strange animal behavior is reported.

Residents experience unexplained symptoms.

**Expansion Stage**

Human infections appear.

Rot organisms emerge.

Environmental growth structures begin spreading.

**Integration Stage**

Large biological structures appear.

Entire buildings or landscapes become fused with Rot tissue.

The area begins to function as a self-contained ecosystem.

---

# 9. Franchise Narrative Structure

The ROT franchise follows an expanding narrative structure.

Each entry introduces a new outbreak zone while revealing additional information about the Rot phenomenon.

The long-term story progresses through several narrative layers.

---

## 9.1 Local Disaster

Early stories focus on individual outbreak zones.

Players experience events through the perspective of characters directly trapped inside these disasters.

The primary goal is survival and escape.

---

## 9.2 Scientific Investigation

As the franchise progresses, the origins of the Rot begin to emerge.

Research logs, recovered data, and survivor accounts reveal the growing scientific understanding of the phenomenon.

The role of organizations like Halcyon becomes clearer.

---

## 9.3 Global Implications

Eventually, the story expands beyond isolated outbreaks.

Evidence suggests the Rot may exist across multiple locations worldwide.

The phenomenon may represent a planetary biological system rather than a localized anomaly.

---

# 10. Long-Term Franchise Questions

The ROT franchise gradually explores several major questions.

These mysteries drive the overarching narrative across multiple games.

Key questions include:

- Where did the Rot originate?
- How long has it existed on Earth?
- Why does the Rot activate in certain locations?
- Is the Rot truly destructive, or is it part of a natural ecological system?
- Can humanity learn to coexist with the Rot?

These questions are not intended to be answered immediately.

Instead, the franchise slowly reveals fragments of information across multiple stories.

---

# 11. Franchise Structure

ROT is designed as a **multi-entry franchise** rather than a single isolated story.

Each game focuses on a new outbreak zone and new characters.

While individual stories remain self-contained, the broader narrative continues to expand across entries.

Future games may explore:

- new geographic regions
- different environmental ecosystems
- additional Rot organism types
- deeper revelations about the Rot network

This structure allows the universe to grow while maintaining narrative mystery.

---

# 12. Canon Rules

To maintain consistency across the franchise, several canonical rules apply to all ROT entries.

---

## 12.1 The Rot Is Biological

The Rot is always presented as a biological phenomenon rather than supernatural magic.

Even when its origins remain mysterious, it behaves according to biological and ecological principles.

---

## 12.2 Human Knowledge Is Limited

Human understanding of the Rot is incomplete.

Even scientists studying the phenomenon rarely possess the full picture.

This limitation allows the story to preserve mystery.

---

## 12.3 Outbreak Zones Are Isolated

Each outbreak zone is typically geographically isolated.

This allows the Rot ecosystem to develop without immediate global exposure.

Isolation also reinforces the survival horror atmosphere.

---

## 12.4 Characters Are Not Superheroes

Protagonists remain grounded individuals rather than military super-soldiers.

Survival depends on observation, planning, and resource management rather than overwhelming firepower.
