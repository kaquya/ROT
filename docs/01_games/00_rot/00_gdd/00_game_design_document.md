# ROT – Game Design Document

Version: 1.0  
Project Status: Pre-Production

---

# 1. Game Overview

ROT is a narrative-driven survival horror game set in the coastal town of **Blackwater Cove**, where an ancient biological system known as **the Rot** has begun restructuring the environment and the organisms within it.

Players explore interconnected districts while searching for resources, solving environmental puzzles, and surviving encounters with organisms reconstructed by the Rot ecosystem.

The game combines:

• exploration-driven survival horror  
• environmental storytelling  
• tactical resource management  
• biological horror grounded in ecological themes

Estimated playtime: **10–12 hours**

---

# 2. Player Experience

ROT is designed to create a constant feeling of tension and discovery.

The player should experience:

• isolation within a quarantined environment  
• uncertainty about the nature of the Rot phenomenon  
• fear of encountering unfamiliar organisms  
• relief when discovering safe zones

The player is vulnerable but capable of surviving through careful observation and planning.

Combat exists as a defensive tool rather than the dominant gameplay strategy.

---

# 3. Core Gameplay Pillars

The design of ROT is built around five major pillars.

---

## Exploration

Players explore interconnected districts of Blackwater Cove.

Exploration involves:

• searching buildings and environments  
• discovering shortcuts and new routes  
• locating supplies and key items  
• uncovering narrative clues

The environment itself serves as the primary storytelling medium.

---

## Survival

Resources are intentionally limited.

Players must carefully manage:

• ammunition  
• healing supplies  
• crafting materials  
• inventory space

Every encounter requires evaluating whether combat is necessary.

---

## Environmental Storytelling

The story is primarily revealed through the world itself.

Examples include:

• abandoned vehicles  
• survivor barricades  
• research documents  
• biological transformation of environments

Players reconstruct the outbreak timeline through exploration.

---

## Tactical Combat

Combat is dangerous and resource-driven.

The player must consider:

• enemy positioning  
• available ammunition  
• environmental hazards

Avoiding combat is often the safest option.

---

## Ecological Horror

The Rot ecosystem transforms living organisms into distorted biological reconstructions.

Creatures encountered in the game represent attempts by the Rot system to rebuild ecological roles using incomplete biological memories.

The horror comes from the realization that these organisms were once ordinary life.

---

# 4. Protagonist

The player controls **Elena Voss**, a search-and-recovery diver returning to Blackwater Cove after receiving a fragmented distress message from her younger brother.

Her profession has placed her in dangerous underwater environments, giving her a calm and methodical approach to survival situations.

However, Elena is not a trained soldier.

Her survival depends on observation and careful decision making rather than brute force.

---

# 5. Narrative Premise

Elena arrives in Blackwater Cove expecting to investigate strange biological material discovered in fishing nets.

Instead she finds the town under emergency quarantine.

Most residents have vanished.

Those who remain have been transformed by the Rot ecosystem.

Cut off from the outside world, Elena begins searching for her brother while uncovering the events that led to the outbreak.

Her investigation reveals the involvement of a scientific organization known as **the Halcyon Initiative**, which had been studying the Rot organism before containment failed.

---

# 6. Setting

The game takes place entirely within **Blackwater Cove**, a small coastal town surrounded by cliffs, forest, and deep ocean waters.

The town developed around fishing and maritime industries.

Its geography naturally isolates it from surrounding regions.

This isolation helps explain why the outbreak initially remained contained.

As the Rot spreads, the environment gradually transforms from a recognizable town into a biological ecosystem.

---

# 7. World Structure

Blackwater Cove is divided into interconnected districts.

These districts form a semi-open world that players explore throughout the game.

Major districts include:

• Residential District  
• Downtown District  
• Emergency Coordination Center  
• Harbor District  
• Hospital  
• Forest Region  
• Halcyon Marine Research Center

The final area lies beneath the research center in a cavern system known as the **Rot Convergence Chamber**.

---

# 8. Gameplay Loop

The core gameplay loop repeats throughout the game:

Explore environments  
Observe environmental clues  
Avoid or confront enemies  
Solve environmental puzzles  
Collect key items and resources  
Return to safe zones to prepare  
Advance deeper into the world

This loop gradually reveals the story while increasing the difficulty of encounters.

---

# 9. Exploration Systems

Exploration drives progression in ROT.

Players search environments for:

• supplies  
• crafting materials  
• key items  
• narrative documents

Hidden areas reward players who thoroughly investigate their surroundings.

Exploration often leads to discovering shortcuts that connect districts together.

---

# 10. Safe Zones

Safe zones provide temporary refuge within dangerous environments.

Players can:

• save progress  
• access storage containers  
• craft survival items  
• review collected documents

Enemies cannot enter safe zones.

These locations provide moments of relief between exploration segments.

---

# 11. Combat System

Combat in ROT is designed to support survival tension rather than action dominance.

The player is capable of defending themselves but is rarely powerful enough to eliminate every threat encountered.

Encounters are intentionally dangerous.

Poor positioning or reckless ammunition use can quickly lead to death.

Combat therefore exists as a **strategic tool for survival rather than a primary gameplay method**.

---

## Combat Principles

Combat design follows three key principles.

Scarcity

Weapons and ammunition are limited. Players must decide when combat is necessary.

Vulnerability

The protagonist is not a trained soldier. Enemies can overwhelm the player if approached recklessly.

Tactical Decision Making

Each encounter should present multiple choices:

• fight  
• avoid  
• retreat  
• use environmental hazards

---

## Weapon Categories

Weapons fall into three primary categories.

### Handgun

The most common weapon.

Characteristics:

• moderate damage  
• good accuracy  
• relatively common ammunition

The handgun serves as the player's primary defensive weapon.

---

### Shotgun

A powerful close-range weapon.

Characteristics:

• high damage  
• slow reload speed  
• limited ammunition

The shotgun is useful for dealing with large enemies or close encounters.

---

### Hunting Rifle

A long-range precision weapon.

Characteristics:

• very high damage  
• slow firing rate  
• rare ammunition

The rifle rewards careful aiming at enemy weak points.

---

# 12. Inventory System

Inventory management is a central survival mechanic.

The player operates with a limited number of inventory slots.

All items share the same inventory space.

This includes:

• weapons  
• ammunition  
• healing supplies  
• crafting materials  
• key items

Because space is limited, players must constantly evaluate which items are worth carrying.

---

## Storage Containers

Storage containers are located in safe zones.

Players can deposit items they do not need immediately.

All containers share the same storage inventory, allowing items to be retrieved from any safe zone.

---

# 13. Crafting System

Crafting allows players to create survival items using materials discovered during exploration.

Crafting does not eliminate resource scarcity.

Instead it provides limited flexibility in how players manage supplies.

Crafting can only be performed in safe zones or designated workbenches.

---

## Craftable Items

Examples include:

Bandages

Restore small amounts of health.

Medical Injection

Restore larger amounts of health.

Antitoxin Compound

Reduces damage from Rot contamination.

Improvised Ammunition

Creates small amounts of ammunition using scavenged materials.

---

# 14. The Rot Ecosystem

The Rot is not a traditional infection.

It behaves more like a distributed biological ecosystem.

Instead of simply infecting hosts, the Rot reorganizes biological matter.

Different environments produce different types of Rot organisms.

Examples include:

• urban Rot organisms formed from humans  
• marine Rot organisms near coastal areas  
• wildlife Rot organisms in forest ecosystems

This adaptability makes the Rot ecosystem increasingly complex as it spreads.

---

# 15. Enemy Types

Creatures encountered in the game represent different outcomes of the Rot reconstruction process.

---

## Rotters

Rotters are the most common enemies.

They originate from infected human hosts.

Characteristics:

• slow movement  
• unstable body structures  
• aggressive behavior

While individually weak, Rotters become dangerous in groups.

---

## Collapsed Rotters

Collapsed Rotters represent advanced stages of mutation.

Their skeletal structure has partially collapsed under Rot growth.

They move using crawling or dragging motions.

These enemies often ambush the player in confined spaces.

---

## Harbor Rotters

Harbor Rotters appear near marine environments.

Their bodies are fused with marine organisms.

They are typically more durable than standard Rotters.

---

## Medical Rotters

These enemies originate from infected hospital patients.

Experimental treatments attempted during the outbreak caused unusual mutations.

They appear primarily in hospital environments.

---

## Stalkers

Stalkers are predator-type Rot organisms.

They move quickly and actively hunt the player.

Two variants appear in the game:

Shadow Stalkers

Ambush predators found in dark interior environments.

Hunter Stalkers

Faster predators encountered outdoors.

---

# 16. Enemy AI

Enemy behavior is built around simple but dynamic systems.

Enemies respond to environmental stimuli rather than scripted events.

---

## Awareness States

Enemies operate according to awareness states.

Dormant

Inactive until disturbed.

Idle

Wandering within a limited area.

Suspicious

Investigating unusual sounds or movement.

Aggressive

Actively attacking the player.

---

## Detection

Enemies detect the player using two senses.

Sight

Movement within the enemy’s field of view.

Sound

Noise generated by running, combat, or interacting with objects.

Players can reduce detection risk by moving slowly and avoiding loud actions.

---

# 17. Boss Encounters

Major bosses represent large-scale organisms formed when the Rot ecosystem reaches advanced development stages.

Boss fights emphasize environmental interaction rather than pure combat.

Players defeat bosses by:

• destroying biological nodes  
• activating environmental hazards  
• targeting exposed weak points

---

## Major Bosses

Harbor Bloom Guardian

A massive Rot organism fused with harbor machinery.

Spliced Medical Mutation

A multi-host organism formed during failed hospital experiments.

Dr Victor Soren

The final boss, a Halcyon researcher who merged himself with the Rot ecosystem.

---

# 18. Puzzle Design

Puzzles in ROT are grounded in environmental logic.

Players restore or interact with real-world systems rather than solving abstract puzzles.

Examples include:

• restoring electrical power  
• repairing mechanical systems  
• locating access cards  
• removing Rot growth blocking doors

Puzzle complexity increases gradually throughout the game.
