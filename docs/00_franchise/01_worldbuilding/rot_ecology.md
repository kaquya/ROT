## 1. Overview

The Rot is the central biological phenomenon of the ROT universe. It is not a virus, parasite, or conventional disease. Instead, it behaves as a biological restructuring system that reorganizes life into new ecological forms.

When the Rot enters an environment, it does not simply infect organisms. It alters biological structures, reshapes ecological relationships, and reorganizes entire ecosystems.

The Rot transforms living organisms into new forms that fulfill specific ecological functions within a reorganized biological hierarchy.

Understanding the Rot as a system rather than an infection is essential for maintaining the identity of the ROT universe.

---

## 2. Nature of the Rot

The Rot operates as a biological force that restructures living systems.

Instead of spreading through simple contagion, the Rot introduces changes that propagate through biological interaction, environmental integration, and ecosystem adaptation.

The Rot affects three primary domains:

- organisms  
- environments  
- ecological relationships  

Once established in a region, the Rot begins reorganizing life according to new biological patterns.

---

## 3. Biological Restructuring

The Rot restructures organisms by altering anatomy, physiology, and biological purpose.

These changes do not occur randomly. Mutations appear to follow patterns that support the development of stable ecosystems.

Examples of restructuring include:

- skeletal and muscular adaptation for new mobility patterns  
- growth of plant-like or fungal tissue across animal structures  
- fusion of multiple biological systems into hybrid organisms  
- repurposing of existing organs for new environmental roles  

These transformations produce organisms that appear unnatural to human observers but function effectively within the new ecosystem.

---

## 4. Mutation Behavior

Mutations caused by the Rot follow several consistent characteristics.

### Adaptive Mutation

Changes often appear to support ecological survival. Organisms gain traits that improve their ability to hunt, defend, reproduce, or integrate with the environment.

### Rapid Transformation

The rate of mutation is significantly faster than natural evolutionary processes. Organisms may change dramatically within short time periods.

### Structural Integration

Mutations frequently integrate biological systems that would normally remain separate. Examples include plant tissue merging with animal anatomy or fungal growth reinforcing skeletal structures.

### Environmental Compatibility

Mutated organisms often display traits that match their environment, suggesting that the Rot adapts biological forms to environmental conditions.

---

## 5. Ecosystem Hierarchy

Once the Rot establishes itself in a region, a new ecological hierarchy begins to emerge.

These ecosystems often include multiple organism types that fulfill distinct roles.

Common ecological roles within Rot environments include:

### Scavengers

Small or mobile organisms that consume organic waste and dead tissue.

### Predators

Aggressive organisms that hunt other creatures within the Rot ecosystem.

### Parasites

Organisms that attach to hosts and draw nutrients from them.

### Environmental Growth

Stationary biological structures that reshape terrain and environments.

### Apex Mutations

Large and highly specialized organisms that dominate the ecosystem.

These roles allow Rot ecosystems to maintain internal biological balance.

---

## 6. Environmental Integration

The Rot does not only affect living creatures. It also integrates with physical environments.

Structures, terrain, and vegetation may become partially biological after prolonged exposure.

Examples of environmental integration include:

- biological growth spreading across buildings and infrastructure  
- plant or fungal structures forming inside collapsed environments  
- organic materials reinforcing or reshaping terrain  
- hybrid environments where biological and artificial structures merge  

Over time, environments affected by the Rot begin to resemble living systems rather than abandoned human spaces.

---

## 7. Environmental Spread

The Rot spreads through multiple mechanisms.

Unlike traditional infection models, spread is influenced by environmental conditions and biological interaction.

Common spread mechanisms include:

### Biological Contact

Organisms already affected by the Rot can introduce the system into new populations.

### Environmental Contamination

Areas heavily affected by the Rot may produce spores, organic residue, or biological growth that spreads into surrounding regions.

### Ecosystem Expansion

As mutated organisms migrate or expand their territory, the Rot ecosystem grows into adjacent environments.

### Structural Growth

Rot-based biological structures may expand physically across terrain, gradually overtaking new areas.

Spread tends to occur gradually rather than explosively, allowing ecosystems to stabilize as they expand.

---

## 8. Stability of Rot Ecosystems

Rot ecosystems eventually reach a form of biological stability.

After a period of rapid mutation and environmental restructuring, ecosystems develop relatively stable relationships between organisms.

Predator and prey populations begin to balance.

Environmental growth slows once available resources are integrated into the ecosystem.

This stability suggests that the Rot is capable of forming sustainable ecological systems rather than endlessly destructive environments.

---

## 9. Interaction with Human Systems

Human environments provide large amounts of structural material and biological resources.

Urban environments affected by the Rot often experience the following transformations:

- buildings partially converted into biological structures  
- abandoned infrastructure used as habitat for mutated organisms  
- research facilities overtaken by biological growth  
- containment attempts that unintentionally accelerate mutation  

Human intervention often destabilizes Rot ecosystems, producing unpredictable mutation events.

---

## 10. Unknown Aspects

Despite extensive study by researchers within the ROT universe, many aspects of the Rot remain unexplained.

Unknown factors include:

- the origin of the Rot  
- the underlying mechanism that drives biological restructuring  
- the limits of mutation potential  
- the long term evolutionary outcome of Rot ecosystems  

These unknown elements remain an important part of the ROT universe.

Maintaining scientific uncertainty ensures that the Rot continues to feel mysterious and unsettling.

## 11. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the biological logic behind the Rot and its ecosystems.

It provides a framework for designing creatures, environments, and narrative elements within the ROT universe.

All biological transformations, mutated organisms, and environmental changes should follow the principles described here to maintain consistency across the franchise.
