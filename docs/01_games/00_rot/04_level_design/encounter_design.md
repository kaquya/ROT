# 1. Overview

Encounter design defines how players experience danger, tension, and confrontation while exploring the world of Blackwater Cove.

Encounters in ROT are not simply combat sequences. They represent moments where the player interacts with the ecosystem through threat, uncertainty, and environmental pressure.

Encounters must support the core gameplay pillars:

- survival horror tension  
- ecological horror  
- exploration  
- narrative discovery  

Encounters should feel like the player has entered a **living ecosystem** rather than a scripted fight arena.

---

# 2. Encounter Design Philosophy

Encounter design follows several key principles.

## Uncertainty

Players should rarely know exactly when danger will occur. Encounters should emerge naturally from the environment.

## Environmental Integration

Encounters should feel tied to the location where they occur. Creature placement must reflect ecological behavior.

## Player Choice

Players should usually have multiple ways to react:

- engage in combat  
- avoid enemies through stealth  
- retreat from the area  
- use the environment for survival

## Resource Pressure

Encounters should force players to consider their limited resources.

---

# 3. Encounter Types

Encounters in ROT fall into several categories.

---

# 4. Combat Encounters

Combat encounters involve direct confrontation with hostile organisms.

These encounters test the player’s ability to manage resources, position themselves effectively, and react to enemy behavior.

## Characteristics

Combat encounters often include:

- multiple enemies  
- limited maneuvering space  
- environmental hazards  
- resource pressure

## Example Situations

Examples of combat encounters include:

- enemies guarding narrow alleyways  
- predators occupying territorial zones  
- groups of scavengers feeding in contaminated areas

Combat encounters should never feel excessive. Players should always consider whether fighting is worth the cost.

---

# 5. Tension Events

Tension events create psychological pressure without immediate combat.

These moments increase player anxiety and anticipation.

## Examples

Tension events may include:

- distant creature sounds echoing through empty corridors  
- movement detected in the environment but no visible enemy  
- environmental objects shifting unexpectedly  
- sudden silence after prolonged ambient noise

Tension events help maintain suspense during exploration.

---

# 6. Ambush Encounters

Ambush encounters occur when enemies attack unexpectedly.

These encounters are designed to surprise the player and create sudden danger.

## Ambush Triggers

Ambushes may occur when:

- the player opens a door or enters a room  
- environmental objects collapse or break  
- enemies emerge from hidden locations  
- the player activates machinery or power systems

## Design Guidelines

Ambush encounters should be used sparingly.

Too many ambushes reduce their effectiveness and make encounters predictable.

---

# 7. Environmental Encounters

Some encounters arise from the environment rather than direct enemy attacks.

Examples include:

- Rot growth releasing spores  
- collapsing structures  
- biological hazards spreading through rooms

Environmental encounters emphasize that the environment itself can be dangerous.

---

# 8. Encounter Placement

Encounters should reflect the ecological logic of the Rot ecosystem.

Examples include:

- scavengers near organic remains  
- predators guarding territory  
- parasites emerging from infected hosts

Enemies should rarely appear in locations where they would not logically exist.

---

# 9. Encounter Density

Enemy density should scale gradually throughout the game.

## Early Game

Encounters are limited and predictable.

The player learns core mechanics.

## Mid Game

Enemy variety increases and encounters become more complex.

## Late Game

Encounters involve stronger enemies and dangerous environments.

Enemy groups may combine different creature types.

---

# 10. Encounter Flow

Encounters should follow a natural flow.

Typical encounter structure:

Exploration → Suspicion → Encounter → Resolution

The player should feel tension building before a full encounter occurs.

---

# 11. Integration with Other Systems

Encounter design interacts with multiple gameplay systems.

Examples include:

- Enemy AI System for creature behavior  
- Stealth System for detection mechanics  
- Combat System for player engagement  
- Audio System for tension building  

Level design must support these systems by providing appropriate spaces for encounters.

---

# 12. Purpose of This Document

This document defines how encounters are designed within ROT.

By balancing combat encounters, tension events, and ambush situations, encounter design maintains suspense and reinforces the survival horror atmosphere of the game.

Encounters should always feel connected to the environment and the evolving Rot ecosystem.
