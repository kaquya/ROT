# Gameplay Flow

This document defines the **moment-to-moment gameplay loop** of ROT.

The goal of the gameplay flow is to create a **consistent survival horror rhythm** that balances:

- exploration
- tension
- combat
- resource management
- narrative discovery

The player should constantly move between **danger, relief, and discovery**.

---

# Core Gameplay Loop

The fundamental gameplay loop of ROT is built around exploration and survival.

```
Explore Area
↓
Encounter Threat
↓
Player Decision
    Fight
    Stealth
    Escape
↓
Resource Management
↓
Solve Environmental Challenge
↓
Unlock Shortcut or Progression
↓
Reach Safe Zone
↓
Prepare for Next Area
```

This loop repeats across every district in the game.

Each cycle increases tension by introducing stronger enemies and more complex challenges.

---

# Exploration Phase

Exploration is the primary activity of the player.

Players search environments for:

- items
- ammunition
- crafting materials
- lore documents
- puzzle components
- hidden shortcuts

Exploration spaces are designed with **interconnected routes and loops**.

This encourages players to:

- revisit locations
- unlock shortcuts
- discover hidden supplies

Environmental storytelling plays a large role during exploration.

Players learn about the outbreak through:

- abandoned buildings
- personal belongings
- destroyed infrastructure
- Rot corruption patterns

---

# Threat Encounter

While exploring, the player will encounter hostile entities.

These encounters may include:

- Rotter groups
- Stalker ambushes
- Spliced elite enemies
- environmental hazards

Enemy placement is designed to create tension without overwhelming the player.

Encounters should encourage decision making rather than constant combat.

---

# Player Decision

When encountering enemies, the player must decide how to respond.

The three primary responses are:

### Fight

The player engages enemies using weapons.

Combat is dangerous and consumes limited resources.

Players must:

- aim carefully
- target weak points
- conserve ammunition

Fighting is sometimes necessary but should rarely be the default choice.

---

### Stealth

The player avoids detection.

Stealth tactics include:

- crouching
- staying in shadows
- avoiding noise
- moving slowly

Stealth is particularly effective against stronger enemies.

---

### Escape

Sometimes the best option is retreat.

Players may:

- run to a safer area
- break line of sight
- use environmental obstacles

Escaping enemies allows players to conserve resources for later encounters.

---

# Resource Management

After encounters, players must manage their supplies.

This includes:

- healing injuries
- reloading weapons
- crafting items
- organizing inventory space

Inventory space is intentionally limited.

Players must choose which items to carry and which to leave behind.

Resource management adds strategic tension to exploration.

---

# Environmental Challenges

Progression through the game often requires solving environmental challenges.

These challenges may include:

- locked doors
- mechanical puzzles
- power restoration systems
- blocked pathways

Solving these challenges often unlocks new areas of the map.

Puzzles are designed to encourage exploration rather than halt gameplay entirely.

---

# Shortcuts and Map Progression

As players explore, they will unlock shortcuts connecting different parts of the map.

Shortcuts reduce backtracking distance and help players navigate the environment more efficiently.

Examples include:

- unlocked gates
- broken barriers
- newly opened pathways
- ladder shortcuts

This system creates interconnected levels similar to classic survival horror games.

---

# Safe Zones

Safe zones provide temporary relief from danger.

Safe zones allow the player to:

- save progress
- store items
- craft supplies
- reorganize inventory

Safe zones are intentionally placed between high tension areas.

This pacing creates a cycle of stress and relief.

---

# Narrative Discovery

Throughout the game, players discover lore through environmental storytelling and collectibles.

Collectibles may include:

- personal notes
- police reports
- medical records
- Halcyon research files

These documents gradually reveal the truth behind the Rot outbreak.

Players who explore thoroughly will uncover deeper layers of the story.

---

# Escalation of Difficulty

As the game progresses, the gameplay loop becomes more dangerous.

Changes include:

- stronger enemy types
- increased enemy density
- more complex puzzles
- larger exploration spaces

The player gradually gains access to better weapons and resources.

However, the Rot threat continues to escalate.

---

# Boss Encounters

Boss encounters occur at key narrative milestones.

These encounters introduce large or unique enemies with specialized mechanics.

Boss fights often involve:

- environmental hazards
- multi-phase combat
- puzzle elements within the arena

Defeating a boss typically unlocks a new region of the game world.

---

# Endgame Flow

The final sections of the game increase intensity by combining multiple systems.

Players must:

- navigate complex environments
- fight advanced enemies
- manage scarce resources
- uncover the truth behind the Rot

The final confrontation represents the climax of both the narrative and gameplay systems.

---

# Summary

The gameplay flow of ROT revolves around repeating cycles of:

- exploration
- tension
- decision making
- discovery

Players are constantly balancing risk and reward as they navigate the infected world of Blackwater Cove.

This loop ensures that the game maintains tension while encouraging exploration and strategic thinking.
