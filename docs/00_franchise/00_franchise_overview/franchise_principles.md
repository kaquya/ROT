# ROT Franchise Principles

Project: ROT  
Version: 1.1  
Document Type: Franchise Principles  
Scope: ROT Universe  
Status: Core Reference  

---

## 1. Overview

This document defines the creative principles that guide all projects set in the ROT universe.

The purpose of these principles is to ensure that every game, narrative, or expansion within the franchise maintains a consistent identity. While individual projects may explore different locations, characters, or stages of the Rot phenomenon, the core tone and design philosophy must remain recognizable.

These principles define how horror is presented, how stories are told, and how the Rot should be interpreted within the universe.

---

## 2. Tone Guidelines

The ROT universe is defined by a tone of quiet tension, scientific curiosity, and environmental decay.

The world should feel abandoned but not empty. Life continues to exist, but it has changed in ways that are unsettling and unfamiliar.

Environments should communicate the sense that human civilization has been interrupted rather than completely destroyed. Evidence of former human activity remains present throughout the world.

Key tone characteristics include:

- melancholic atmosphere
- environmental silence and isolation
- slow biological takeover of human spaces
- remnants of human research and containment efforts
- subtle but constant signs of ecological change

The tone should avoid exaggerated or theatrical horror. Fear should emerge gradually through discovery and environmental observation.

---

## 3. Horror Philosophy

The ROT franchise focuses on biological and ecological horror rather than supernatural horror.

Creatures and environments should feel like the result of natural processes, even when those processes are unfamiliar or disturbing.

The horror should come from recognizing familiar elements that have been transformed by the Rot.

Examples include:

- wildlife mutated into new ecological roles
- plant growth merging with human structures
- environments reorganized into unfamiliar ecosystems
- organisms adapting to new biological hierarchies

The Rot should never appear as a chaotic or purely destructive force. Instead, it should behave like a system that reorganizes life according to new biological rules.

This creates a form of horror that feels plausible and grounded.

---

## 4. Narrative Approach

Stories in the ROT universe should emphasize discovery rather than direct explanation.

Information about the Rot, the world, and past events should be revealed gradually through exploration.

Narratives should rely heavily on environmental storytelling. The world itself should contain clues about what happened before the player's arrival.

Examples of narrative delivery include:

- research logs and scientific notes
- abandoned laboratories and facilities
- personal journals and recordings
- environmental evidence of experiments or containment attempts
- mutated ecosystems that reveal how the Rot spreads and adapts

The audience should assemble the story by observing and interpreting the environment.

Direct exposition should be used sparingly.

---

## 5. Design Philosophy

Design within the ROT universe should support tension, uncertainty, and exploration.

Gameplay systems should encourage players to observe their surroundings and learn how the Rot has transformed the environment.

Important design priorities include:

- limited resources that encourage careful decision making
- exploration focused progression
- environmental puzzles and barriers
- encounters that emphasize survival rather than domination
- ecosystems that influence enemy behavior

The player should feel vulnerable within the world, but not powerless. Success should come from understanding the environment and adapting to the Rot ecosystem.

---

## 6. The Rot as a Natural System

The Rot must always be portrayed as a biological system rather than a traditional infection.

It does not simply spread through contamination. Instead, it restructures organisms and environments.

Key principles for portraying the Rot include:

- mutation serves ecological functions
- organisms adapt to fill new environmental roles
- biological structures grow and integrate with existing life
- new predator and prey relationships emerge

The Rot should feel like an evolving ecosystem rather than a disease outbreak.

Maintaining this perspective ensures that the franchise retains its unique identity.

---

## 7. Environmental Storytelling

The environment is one of the primary storytelling tools in the ROT universe.

Locations should contain visual evidence of past events and ongoing ecological change.

Examples include:

- abandoned research sites
- partially evacuated towns
- containment zones overtaken by biological growth
- experimental facilities damaged by uncontrolled mutations

Players should be able to infer narrative details by observing these environments.

Environmental storytelling should complement written or recorded narrative fragments rather than replace them.

---

## 8. Mystery Preservation

The Rot should never be fully explained.

Scientific institutions within the universe may attempt to study and categorize the Rot, but their understanding should always remain incomplete.

Maintaining uncertainty allows the Rot to remain unsettling and unpredictable.

New discoveries may reveal additional aspects of the Rot, but the phenomenon itself should remain partially unknown.

This ensures that the franchise can continue expanding without removing the sense of mystery.

---

## 9. Consistency Across Projects

All ROT projects should follow the principles described in this document.

Different games or stories may focus on different characters, regions, or stages of the Rot phenomenon. However, the underlying rules of the world should remain consistent.

Consistency ensures that the ROT universe feels like a coherent setting rather than a collection of unrelated stories.

---

## 10. Purpose of This Document

This document acts as a creative guideline for all ROT franchise development.

It ensures that the tone, narrative approach, and biological identity of the Rot remain consistent across future projects.

By following these principles, the franchise can expand into new stories and locations while maintaining a recognizable and cohesive world.
