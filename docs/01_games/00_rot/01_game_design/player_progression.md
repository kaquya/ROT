## 1. Overview

Player progression in ROT is driven by exploration, knowledge, and gradual access to new areas and tools. Unlike traditional progression systems that rely heavily on character leveling or skill trees, ROT focuses on environmental discovery and equipment acquisition.

Progression is designed to feel grounded within the world. Players advance by understanding their surroundings, uncovering narrative context, and gaining access to tools that allow them to navigate increasingly dangerous environments.

The progression system operates across three primary layers:

- exploration progression  
- narrative progression  
- equipment progression  

These layers reinforce each other throughout the game.

---

## 2. Exploration Progression

Exploration progression determines how players move through the world and gain access to new areas.

Blackwater Cove is divided into multiple districts and environmental zones. Early areas are partially contaminated but remain relatively navigable. As players progress, they encounter environments that show deeper Rot transformation and greater environmental hazards.

Exploration progression is typically gated through environmental barriers such as:

- locked doors or secured facilities  
- collapsed structures that require alternate routes  
- contaminated zones that require protective equipment  
- blocked pathways that can only be accessed after acquiring tools  

As players discover keys, tools, or environmental shortcuts, previously inaccessible areas become available.

This structure encourages players to revisit earlier locations with new knowledge or equipment.

---

## 3. Narrative Progression

Narrative progression occurs as players uncover fragments of the story through exploration.

The narrative is delivered through environmental storytelling and scattered research materials rather than direct exposition.

Players may discover narrative information through:

- research logs from scientific facilities  
- personal notes from former residents  
- abandoned laboratories and containment sites  
- environmental scenes that reveal past events  

Narrative discoveries help players understand the events that led to the transformation of Blackwater Cove and the role of scientific research in studying the Rot.

As the story unfolds, players gain a clearer understanding of the Rot ecosystem and the consequences of failed containment efforts.

However, the full nature of the Rot remains partially unknown.

---

## 4. Equipment Progression

Equipment progression provides players with tools that improve their ability to survive and navigate Rot affected environments.

Instead of traditional character upgrades, progression is primarily tied to equipment and tools found throughout the world.

Examples of equipment upgrades may include:

- improved flashlights or lighting equipment  
- protective gear that reduces Rot exposure  
- tools used to access sealed or damaged structures  
- expanded inventory capacity  

Equipment progression allows players to explore increasingly dangerous areas while maintaining the survival focused design philosophy.

---

## 5. Knowledge as Progression

Knowledge gained by the player also functions as a form of progression.

As players encounter different Rot organisms and environmental hazards, they begin to recognize patterns in creature behavior and ecosystem structure.

This understanding allows players to navigate environments more safely and anticipate potential dangers.

Knowledge progression reinforces the investigative nature of the game.

---

## 6. Increasing Environmental Complexity

As progression continues, environments become more biologically transformed by the Rot.

Players encounter:

- denser Rot ecosystems  
- more aggressive or complex organisms  
- hazardous environmental structures  
- larger contaminated zones  

This gradual increase in environmental complexity reinforces the sense that the player is moving deeper into Rot territory.

---

## 7. Balance Between Challenge and Discovery

Player progression should maintain a balance between challenge and discovery.

New areas should feel more dangerous but also more rewarding to explore.

Players should feel a growing sense of understanding about the Rot ecosystem while still encountering new and unpredictable elements.

This balance maintains the tension and curiosity that define the ROT experience.

---

## 8. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the high level structure of player progression in ROT.

It ensures that exploration, narrative discovery, and equipment upgrades work together to guide the player through the game world.

Maintaining these progression systems helps support the survival horror identity of the game while encouraging investigation and discovery.
