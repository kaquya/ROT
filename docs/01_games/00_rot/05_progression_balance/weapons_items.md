# 1. Overview

This document defines the categories of weapons and usable items available to the player in ROT.

Weapons and items support survival gameplay by providing tools for combat, healing, exploration, and puzzle interaction. Because ROT emphasizes limited resources and tactical decision making, weapons and items must remain scarce and meaningful.

Players should rarely have unlimited access to supplies. Every weapon use or item consumption should feel like a deliberate survival choice.

This document focuses on item categories and design philosophy rather than exact balance numbers.

---

# 2. Weapons and Items Design Philosophy

The design of weapons and items follows several guiding principles.

## Survival Focus

Weapons are tools for survival rather than instruments of power. Players should not feel overwhelmingly strong compared to enemies.

Combat often involves risk and limited resources.

---

## Resource Scarcity

Items should be distributed carefully across the world.

Players must balance:

- using items immediately  
- saving them for future encounters  

Scarcity reinforces tension during exploration.

---

## Environmental Consistency

Weapons and items must make sense within the environment.

Examples include:

- household tools found in homes  
- medical supplies in hospitals  
- industrial tools in harbor facilities  
- research equipment in laboratories  

Items should never appear randomly without context.

---

## Tactical Variety

Different weapons and items should encourage different survival strategies.

Examples include:

- stealth avoidance  
- quick defensive combat  
- environmental manipulation

This variety allows players to adapt to different encounter situations.

---

# 3. Weapon Categories

Weapons in ROT are divided into several categories based on their function.

---

## Melee Weapons

Melee weapons are close-range tools used for direct combat.

Examples include:

- knives  
- improvised tools  
- blunt instruments  
- industrial equipment

Melee combat consumes stamina and exposes the player to danger but does not require ammunition.

These weapons are reliable but risky.

---

## Firearms

Firearms provide ranged combat options.

Examples include:

- handguns  
- shotguns  
- limited rifle weapons

Ammunition for firearms is intentionally limited.

Firearms are powerful but should be used carefully due to scarce ammo supplies.

---

## Improvised Weapons

Improvised weapons are temporary tools created from environmental objects.

Examples include:

- broken pipes  
- construction tools  
- modified equipment

These weapons may have limited durability.

Improvised weapons reinforce the survival theme of the game.

---

# 4. Item Categories

Items support player survival and progression.

---

## Medical Supplies

Medical supplies restore player health.

Examples include:

- bandages  
- first aid kits  
- emergency medical supplies

Healing items should remain limited to preserve tension during exploration.

---

## Utility Items

Utility items assist with exploration and environmental interaction.

Examples include:

- flashlights  
- batteries  
- repair tools  
- navigation equipment

These items may enable access to new areas or improve survival conditions.

---

## Crafting Materials

Crafting materials are resources used in the crafting system.

Examples include:

- scrap metal  
- chemicals  
- mechanical components

Players can combine materials to create useful items.

---

## Key Items

Key items are unique objects required for progression.

Examples include:

- access cards  
- mechanical keys  
- security overrides  
- special tools

Key items typically unlock new areas or activate puzzle systems.

---

# 5. Resource Distribution

Weapons and items are distributed throughout the world to encourage exploration.

Common placement locations include:

- abandoned homes  
- storage rooms  
- research laboratories  
- maintenance areas  
- emergency supply caches

Item placement should support environmental storytelling.

---

# 6. Inventory Interaction

Weapons and items are stored within the player inventory system.

Players must manage limited inventory space.

This creates meaningful decisions about:

- which items to carry  
- which items to discard  
- when to use supplies

Inventory limits reinforce the survival mechanics of the game.

---

# 7. Progression and Equipment

Players may gradually obtain improved equipment during the game.

Examples include:

- stronger weapons  
- more reliable tools  
- upgraded equipment

However, upgrades should remain grounded in the world and should not break the survival horror balance.

---

# 8. Integration with Other Systems

Weapons and items interact with several gameplay systems.

Examples include:

- Combat System for weapon usage  
- Inventory System for storage and management  
- Crafting System for item creation  
- Puzzle System for environmental interaction  

These connections ensure items remain integrated with gameplay progression.

---

# 9. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the categories and design philosophy of weapons and items in ROT.

By maintaining scarcity, environmental consistency, and tactical variety, the weapons and items system reinforces the survival horror experience and encourages careful resource management.
