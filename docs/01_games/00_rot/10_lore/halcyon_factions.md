# 1. Overview

This document defines the internal structure of the Halcyon Initiative and the factions that formed within the organization during the Rot research program.

Although Halcyon officially operates as a scientific research organization, its internal structure became increasingly divided as the Rot research project progressed.

Different groups within Halcyon developed conflicting goals, beliefs, and approaches toward the organism.

These internal divisions played a major role in the eventual collapse of containment at Blackwater Cove.

---

# 2. Halcyon Initiative Structure

The Halcyon Initiative operates as a privately funded research organization specializing in advanced biological and environmental research.

Publicly, Halcyon presents itself as a marine biology and ecological research institution.

Internally, the organization operates through several specialized departments.

These include:

- Executive Directorate  
- Scientific Research Division  
- Containment and Security Division  
- Field Research Division  
- Logistics and Operations  

As the Rot research expanded, informal ideological factions began forming within these departments.

---

# 3. Executive Directorate

The Executive Directorate represents the leadership of the Halcyon Initiative.

This group is responsible for strategic decisions, funding allocation, and research direction.

Members of the Directorate rarely appear directly in field reports or laboratory records.

Instead they influence the project through policy decisions and research approvals.

The Directorate strongly supported continued Rot research despite increasing containment risks.

Their primary motivation appears to be the potential scientific and technological value of the organism.

---

# 4. Scientific Research Division

The Scientific Research Division is responsible for studying the Rot organism and conducting experiments.

This division includes specialists in:

- marine biology  
- genetics  
- cellular restructuring  
- ecological systems  

Many researchers within this division initially viewed the Rot as a groundbreaking biological discovery.

However, as containment incidents increased, opinions within the division began to diverge.

Some scientists advocated for extreme caution, while others believed the organism represented an unprecedented opportunity.

---

# 5. Containment and Security Division

The Containment and Security Division is responsible for maintaining laboratory safety and protecting the research facility.

Their responsibilities include:

- monitoring containment chambers  
- maintaining quarantine protocols  
- responding to biological incidents  
- securing restricted research zones  

Containment staff were often the first to report the dangers posed by the Rot organism.

Many containment personnel warned that the organism displayed unpredictable growth patterns.

Their warnings were frequently ignored by higher level leadership.

---

# 6. Field Research Division

The Field Research Division conducts environmental sampling and ecological studies outside the laboratory.

Field researchers collect biological samples from:

- ocean trench ecosystems  
- coastal environments  
- forest regions surrounding Blackwater Cove

These teams were responsible for recovering the initial Rot samples from deep ocean trench structures.

As the outbreak progressed, field teams also documented environmental contamination spreading beyond the research facility.

---

# 7. Logistics and Operations

The Logistics and Operations department supports the day-to-day functioning of the research facility.

Their responsibilities include:

- facility maintenance  
- equipment management  
- transport of biological samples  
- coordination of research schedules  

Although not directly involved in scientific research, logistics personnel often witnessed the early signs of containment failure.

Some operational reports describe unusual biological growth within facility infrastructure long before the outbreak became widely known.

---

# 8. Emergence of Internal Factions

As the Rot research program advanced, ideological divisions formed within the Halcyon Initiative.

Three major factions gradually emerged.

These factions were not formally recognized departments but rather groups of researchers who shared similar beliefs about how the Rot should be handled.

---

# 9. The Advancement Group

The Advancement Group believed that the Rot represented a revolutionary biological discovery.

Members of this faction advocated continued experimentation despite the risks.

Their goals included:

- understanding the organism’s restructuring abilities  
- harnessing Rot growth for technological purposes  
- studying potential biological adaptation mechanisms  

Many members believed the organism could transform human understanding of biology.

This faction had strong support from the Executive Directorate.

---

# 10. The Containment Advocates

The Containment Advocates believed the organism was too dangerous to study without strict control.

Members of this group pushed for:

- stronger containment procedures  
- suspension of high-risk experiments  
- external oversight of the research program  

Containment advocates often clashed with researchers from the Advancement Group.

Several internal reports suggest that some members attempted to halt experiments shortly before the outbreak.

---

# 11. The Integrationists

A smaller but more extreme faction eventually emerged within the research division.

This group believed the Rot was not merely an organism but an advanced ecological system.

Members of this faction theorized that biological integration with the Rot might allow humans to understand its structure.

Some researchers began experimenting with controlled biological exposure.

These experiments later contributed to the transformation of several individuals within the research facility.

---

# 12. Collapse of Organizational Control

As containment failures increased, the internal structure of the Halcyon Initiative began breaking down.

Communication between departments deteriorated.

Containment teams lost the ability to maintain secure laboratory environments.

Scientific staff became increasingly divided over how to respond.

By the time the outbreak spread beyond the facility, the organization had already lost internal coordination.

---

# 13. Role in Narrative Discovery

The internal conflicts within Halcyon are revealed gradually through collectible records discovered by the player.

Examples include:

- research reports
- internal communications
- incident logs
- personal researcher journals

These documents allow the player to understand how the organization's internal divisions contributed to the disaster.

---

# 14. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the internal structure and ideological divisions of the Halcyon Initiative.

Understanding these factions helps explain the decisions that led to the Rot outbreak and provides narrative context for the events discovered throughout the game world.
