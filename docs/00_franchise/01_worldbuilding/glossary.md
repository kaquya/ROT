# 1. Overview

This glossary defines the canonical terminology used throughout the ROT franchise.

The purpose of this document is to ensure consistent language across all documentation, design discussions, narrative content, and development work.

All teams working on ROT projects should reference these definitions when describing systems, environments, enemies, and narrative elements.

New terminology should be added to this document when new systems or concepts are introduced.

---

# 2. Core Concepts

## Rot

The **Rot** is the central biological phenomenon within the ROT universe.

It is not a traditional disease, infection, or parasite. Instead, the Rot behaves as a **biological restructuring system** capable of reorganizing living organisms and environments into new ecological forms.

The Rot integrates organisms, materials, and environmental structures into a continuously evolving biological ecosystem.

Key characteristics include:

- rapid biological restructuring  
- integration of organisms into new ecological roles  
- growth of large organic structures  
- transformation of environments into biological systems  

The Rot ecosystem operates according to unknown biological rules and may represent an ancient life system.

---

## Rot Exposure

**Rot Exposure** refers to the level of biological contamination affecting a living organism that enters a Rot-infected environment.

In gameplay terms, Rot exposure represents the player’s accumulation of contamination from Rot growth, creatures, or environmental hazards.

High exposure levels can result in:

- physical deterioration  
- hallucinations or psychological effects  
- biological mutation  
- eventual death or transformation

Rot exposure functions as one of the core survival mechanics within the game.

---

## Rot Biomass

**Rot Biomass** refers to organic matter produced by the Rot ecosystem.

This biomass forms the visible structures of the Rot within the environment.

Examples include:

- organic growth on buildings  
- biological tendrils spreading across terrain  
- fleshy environmental structures  
- pulsating organic formations

Rot biomass often spreads across both natural and artificial structures, integrating them into the ecosystem.

---

# 3. Rot Ecosystem Structures

## Convergence Chamber

The **Convergence Chamber** is the central Rot growth zone located beneath the Halcyon Marine Research Facility.

This location represents the largest and most concentrated Rot structure within Blackwater Cove.

Characteristics include:

- massive biological growth formations  
- interconnected organic structures  
- extreme Rot contamination levels  

The Convergence Chamber appears to function as a central node within the Rot ecosystem.

Its true purpose remains unknown.

---

## Rot Growth

**Rot Growth** describes the expansion of biological structures produced by the Rot.

These structures may include:

- organic tunnels  
- branching tendrils  
- layered biological membranes  
- spiral growth patterns  

Rot growth spreads through both natural terrain and artificial environments.

---

## Contamination Zone

A **Contamination Zone** is an area where Rot biomass has significantly altered the environment.

These zones contain high levels of Rot activity and are often dangerous to enter.

Characteristics may include:

- dense organic growth  
- mutated wildlife  
- structural deformation of buildings  
- elevated Rot exposure levels

---

# 4. Creatures

## Rotters

**Rotters** are basic humanoid creatures transformed by the Rot ecosystem.

They represent individuals who were exposed to high levels of Rot contamination.

Characteristics include:

- distorted anatomy  
- aggressive behavior  
- integration with Rot biomass  

Rotters are among the most common enemies encountered within Blackwater Cove.

---

## Stalkers

**Stalkers** are more advanced Rot creatures that exhibit heightened awareness and hunting behavior.

They often remain hidden until an opportunity to ambush prey appears.

Stalkers demonstrate more complex behavior than basic Rotters.

---

## Apex Mutation

An **Apex Mutation** refers to an advanced Rot organism that occupies the highest level of a local Rot ecosystem.

These creatures often act as dominant predators or guardians of Rot growth zones.

Boss creatures in the game often represent apex mutations.

---

# 5. Organizations

## Halcyon Initiative

The **Halcyon Initiative** is a private research organization responsible for the study of the Rot organism.

Publicly, Halcyon presents itself as a marine biology research institute.

Privately, the organization conducted classified biological experiments involving Rot samples recovered from deep ocean trench systems near Blackwater Cove.

Halcyon research eventually led to containment failures that contributed to the Rot outbreak.

---

# 6. Locations

## Blackwater Cove

**Blackwater Cove** is the primary setting of ROT.

It is a small coastal town located along a rugged northern shoreline.

The town developed around a natural harbor used for fishing and maritime industry.

Blackwater Cove eventually becomes the center of the Rot outbreak.

---

## Halcyon Marine Research Facility

The **Halcyon Marine Research Facility** is a research complex located on coastal cliffs overlooking Blackwater Cove.

The facility served as the primary research center for Halcyon’s Rot experiments.

Beneath the facility lies the Convergence Chamber, the largest Rot growth zone discovered in the region.

---

# 7. Narrative Terms

## Environmental Storytelling

**Environmental Storytelling** refers to the method of conveying narrative information through the game world rather than direct exposition.

Examples include:

- abandoned buildings  
- evacuation attempts  
- research documents  
- damaged infrastructure  

Players reconstruct the story of Blackwater Cove through exploration and observation.

---

## Halcyon Records

**Halcyon Records** are collectible narrative documents left behind by researchers working at the Halcyon facility.

These records include:

- research notes  
- incident reports  
- personal journals  
- internal communications  

Collecting these records allows players to uncover the history of Halcyon’s research and the Rot outbreak.
