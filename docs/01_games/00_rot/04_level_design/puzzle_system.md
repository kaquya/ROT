# 1. Overview

The Puzzle System defines how problem solving challenges are integrated into the world of ROT.

Puzzles are used to control progression, encourage exploration, and support environmental storytelling. Rather than feeling like artificial obstacles, puzzles should emerge naturally from the environment and the situations the player encounters.

Puzzle design must remain grounded in the survival horror tone of the game. Most puzzles involve interacting with damaged infrastructure, abandoned systems, or improvised solutions left behind by survivors.

The goal is to create puzzles that feel believable within the world of Blackwater Cove.

---

# 2. Puzzle Design Philosophy

Puzzle design in ROT follows several core principles.

## Environmental Logic

Puzzles should arise naturally from the environment.

Examples include:

- restoring power to a building  
- unlocking mechanical barriers  
- activating damaged equipment  
- navigating blocked pathways  

Players should feel like they are solving real problems rather than abstract game challenges.

---

## Clarity

Players must always have enough information to understand the objective.

Clues may appear through:

- environmental storytelling  
- written documents  
- visual indicators  
- audio cues

The player should never feel completely lost.

---

## Exploration Incentives

Puzzles often encourage players to explore surrounding areas in order to find:

- key items  
- tools  
- access cards  
- environmental mechanisms

This supports the exploration-driven design of the game world.

---

## Narrative Integration

Many puzzles reveal pieces of the story.

Examples include:

- emergency systems activated during the outbreak  
- research equipment used by Halcyon scientists  
- barricades created by survivors

Solving puzzles often reveals new narrative details about the events that occurred in Blackwater Cove.

---

# 3. Puzzle Categories

Puzzles in ROT fall into several main categories.

---

## Mechanical Puzzles

Mechanical puzzles involve interacting with physical systems within the environment.

Examples include:

- activating generators  
- unlocking mechanical doors  
- restoring elevator power  
- adjusting machinery components

These puzzles simulate the repair or operation of damaged infrastructure.

---

## Key Item Puzzles

Key item puzzles require the player to locate specific objects that unlock new areas.

Examples include:

- access cards  
- mechanical keys  
- security overrides  
- specialized tools

These puzzles encourage exploration of surrounding environments.

---

## Environmental Puzzles

Environmental puzzles involve manipulating the environment to progress.

Examples include:

- clearing blocked pathways  
- rerouting power through circuits  
- activating environmental mechanisms  
- navigating through flooded or damaged areas

These puzzles often require observing the environment carefully.

---

## Multi-Step Puzzles

Some puzzles involve several connected actions.

Examples include:

- restoring power to multiple systems  
- activating equipment in a specific order  
- combining multiple key items

Multi-step puzzles often appear in later districts where gameplay complexity increases.

---

# 4. Puzzle Difficulty Progression

Puzzle complexity increases gradually throughout the game.

## Early Game

Puzzles are simple and introduce basic interaction mechanics.

Examples include:

- unlocking doors  
- activating simple switches

## Mid Game

Puzzles become more complex and require exploring larger areas.

Examples include:

- restoring power to multiple rooms  
- locating multiple key items

## Late Game

Puzzles may involve complex environments and multiple puzzle stages.

Examples include:

- laboratory system overrides  
- large facility power restoration

---

# 5. Environmental Clues

Players should be able to discover hints through the environment.

Examples include:

- warning signs  
- maintenance notes  
- survivor messages  
- damaged equipment

These clues help guide the player without explicit instructions.

---

# 6. Puzzle Failure and Recovery

Players should rarely become permanently stuck.

If a puzzle cannot be solved immediately, players should be able to:

- explore other areas  
- collect additional items  
- return later with new information

This supports non-linear exploration.

---

# 7. Integration with Other Systems

The Puzzle System interacts with several gameplay systems.

Examples include:

- the Interaction System for activating objects  
- the Inventory System for key items  
- the Level Design System for puzzle placement  
- the Narrative System for storytelling clues

These systems ensure puzzles remain connected to gameplay and story progression.

---

# 8. Purpose of This Document

This document defines how puzzles function within ROT.

Puzzle design supports exploration, environmental storytelling, and player progression. By grounding puzzles in believable environmental systems, the game maintains immersion while providing meaningful challenges.
