## 1. Overview

This document defines the core rules used to design creatures within the ROT universe.

Creatures in ROT are not traditional monsters. They are organisms that have been reshaped by the Rot ecosystem. Their anatomy, behavior, and ecological role should reflect the biological restructuring principles described in the Rot ecology documentation.

The goal of these rules is to ensure that all creature designs feel consistent with the biological logic of the Rot and maintain the horror identity of the franchise.

ROT creatures should appear unsettling because they feel biologically possible rather than purely fantastical.

---

## 2. Core Design Principles

Creature design within the ROT universe is guided by several core principles.

### Biological Plausibility

All creatures should appear biologically functional.

Even when anatomy is distorted or mutated, the design should suggest how the organism moves, senses its environment, feeds, and survives.

Designs should avoid random mutation that lacks biological purpose.

### Ecological Function

Every creature should appear to fulfill a role within the Rot ecosystem.

Creatures exist as part of a biological hierarchy rather than as isolated monsters.

Each organism should contribute to the ecosystem through behaviors such as scavenging, predation, parasitism, or environmental restructuring.

### Environmental Adaptation

Creature anatomy should reflect the environment where it evolved.

Examples include:

- creatures adapted for movement through dense vegetation  
- organisms that navigate collapsed structures or tight environments  
- predators adapted for coastal or aquatic environments  

Environmental adaptation helps reinforce the realism of Rot ecosystems.

---

## 3. Anatomy Distortion Rules

The Rot alters existing organisms rather than creating entirely new life forms.

Creature designs should therefore retain recognizable elements of the original organism.

This creates a sense of familiarity combined with biological distortion.

### Recognizable Origins

Whenever possible, creature designs should preserve identifiable anatomical features.

Examples include:

- distorted limbs that still resemble the original animal structure  
- skeletal elements that remain visible beneath mutated tissue  
- familiar movement patterns altered by mutation  

This recognition helps players understand that the creature evolved from existing life.

### Functional Mutation

Anatomical distortion should support a biological purpose.

Mutations may include:

- reinforced limbs for improved mobility  
- expanded sensory organs  
- protective biological plating  
- structures that assist with feeding or reproduction  

Designs should suggest how the organism interacts with its environment.

### Structural Integration

The Rot often merges different biological structures together.

Examples include:

- plant-like tissue integrated into animal anatomy  
- fungal growth reinforcing skeletal structures  
- organic membranes forming protective layers  

These integrations should appear natural within the mutated organism.

---

## 4. Ecological Roles

Creatures within Rot ecosystems should occupy specific ecological roles.

These roles help define behavior, movement patterns, and interaction with other organisms.

### Scavengers

Scavenger organisms consume organic waste and biological remains.

They often appear smaller, mobile, and opportunistic.

Scavengers contribute to ecosystem stability by recycling biological material.

### Predators

Predators hunt other organisms within the ecosystem.

Predatory creatures should demonstrate anatomical adaptations that support hunting behavior.

Examples include enhanced sensory structures, powerful limbs, or specialized feeding mechanisms.

### Parasites

Parasitic organisms attach themselves to hosts or environments in order to extract nutrients.

These creatures often appear smaller or physically integrated into other organisms.

### Environmental Organisms

Some creatures exist primarily as stationary biological structures.

These organisms may contribute to environmental growth, resource processing, or ecosystem expansion.

### Apex Organisms

Apex organisms represent the most dominant creatures within a Rot ecosystem.

They are typically larger, more complex, and occupy the highest position in the ecological hierarchy.

---

## 5. Horror Principles

Creature horror in ROT should emerge from biological distortion and ecological realism.

The design should produce discomfort by presenting familiar biological forms that have been reorganized into something unfamiliar.

### Familiar but Distorted

The most effective creature designs combine recognizable anatomy with unsettling modifications.

Examples include:

- limbs that move in unfamiliar ways  
- distorted proportions that remain biologically functional  
- anatomical structures appearing in unexpected locations  

### Subtle Distortion

Horror does not require extreme mutation.

Small distortions of natural anatomy can often produce stronger discomfort than exaggerated transformations.

### Environmental Context

Creatures should appear as part of the surrounding ecosystem.

Their coloration, texture, and biological growth should match the environment where they exist.

This integration reinforces the idea that the creature evolved within the Rot ecosystem.

---

## 6. Movement and Behavior

Creature movement should reflect the organism's anatomy.

Movement patterns should appear physically possible and connected to the creature's biological structure.

Examples include:

- crawling movement supported by multiple limbs  
- slow movement from heavy or reinforced organisms  
- rapid movement from lightweight predators  

Movement should help communicate the creature's ecological role.

---

## 7. Visual Restraint

Creature designs should avoid excessive exaggeration.

Overly chaotic or unrealistic mutations can weaken the biological horror identity of the ROT universe.

Designs should remain grounded in plausible biological structures.

The most effective designs often combine subtle distortion with functional anatomy.

---

## 8. Consistency Across Projects

All creature designs within the ROT franchise should follow the rules defined in this document.

Even when new environments or ecosystems are introduced, creature designs should remain consistent with the biological and ecological logic of the Rot.

Maintaining consistency ensures that creatures across different ROT projects feel like part of the same evolving ecosystem.

---

## 9. Purpose of This Document

This document establishes the design rules used when creating Rot creatures and mutated organisms.

It provides guidance for artists, designers, and writers so that creature designs remain consistent with the biological logic of the ROT universe.

Following these rules ensures that creatures remain believable, unsettling, and integrated into the Rot ecosystem.
