# ROT – Setpiece Locations

Version: 1.0  
Status: Level Design

---

# 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines **major environmental setpiece locations** in ROT.

Setpieces are large, memorable locations designed to:

• create strong visual identity  
• reinforce the atmosphere of Blackwater Cove  
• break up standard exploration areas  
• provide unique gameplay encounters  
• strengthen environmental storytelling  

While the core districts form the structure of the world, setpieces act as **distinct landmarks and narrative highlights**.

A good setpiece should be:

• visually memorable  
• mechanically unique  
• narratively meaningful  

These locations help ensure the world does not feel like a sequence of generic survival horror environments.

---

# 2. Setpiece Design Philosophy

Setpiece locations should follow several principles.

### Visual Distinction

Each setpiece should be immediately recognizable.

Examples:

• unusual architecture  
• large-scale destruction  
• unique environmental lighting  
• dramatic landscape features  

Players should remember these locations long after leaving them.

---

### Gameplay Variation

Setpieces introduce gameplay situations that differ from standard exploration.

Examples:

• vertical navigation  
• environmental hazards  
• large-scale enemy encounters  
• scripted environmental events  

These areas provide pacing variation.

---

### Narrative Reinforcement

Setpieces are often locations where the player learns important information about:

• the Rot phenomenon  
• the Blackwater outbreak  
• Halcyon’s activities  
• the town’s history  

Environmental storytelling should be strong in these areas.

---

# 3. The Rot Aquarium

### Overview

The Rot Aquarium is a partially flooded marine research facility located near the harbor.

Originally built as a public marine exhibit connected to Halcyon's research operations, the aquarium has become heavily contaminated by Rot organisms.

Broken tanks and flooding have allowed marine life to become infected.

---

### Visual Design

Key environmental features:

• shattered glass aquarium tanks  
• flooded corridors  
• massive suspended marine skeletons  
• Rot growth spreading through water systems  
• dim blue lighting from underwater tanks  

Large shadows of mutated creatures occasionally move behind glass walls.

The environment should feel unstable and dangerous.

---

### Gameplay Role

The aquarium serves as a **transition zone between the Harbor District and deeper Rot contamination areas**.

Gameplay elements include:

• narrow flooded corridors  
• broken glass hazards  
• submerged pathways  
• rotating water pumps  

Players may encounter enemies emerging from water.

---

### Enemy Types

Enemies in the aquarium include:

• Harbor Rotters  
• Rot-mutated marine animals  
• Shadow Stalkers hiding in dark corridors  

These enemies use the environment to ambush the player.

---

### Environmental Hazards

Hazards include:

• electrified water sections  
• collapsing aquarium tanks  
• sudden flooding events  

These hazards create tension during combat.

---

### Narrative Elements

Environmental storytelling may reveal:

• Halcyon experiments involving marine Rot samples  
• containment failures in aquatic environments  
• attempts to study Rot adaptation in ocean life  

Several collectible documents may appear in staff offices.

---

# 4. The Collapsed Lighthouse

### Overview

The lighthouse sits on the cliffs overlooking Blackwater Cove.

It once served as a navigation beacon for fishing vessels.

During the outbreak, emergency personnel attempted to use the lighthouse to broadcast distress signals.

Rot contamination eventually destabilized the structure.

Part of the lighthouse has collapsed into the surrounding cliffs.

---

### Visual Design

Key visual elements:

• broken spiral staircase  
• cracked lighthouse tower  
• shattered signal equipment  
• Rot growth spiraling around the tower structure  

The spiral shape of the lighthouse visually echoes the **Rot spiral motif**.

The lighthouse can be seen from several areas of the map.

---

### Gameplay Role

The lighthouse functions as a **vertical exploration area**.

Players climb the partially destroyed tower.

Gameplay elements include:

• narrow staircases  
• collapsing platforms  
• strong coastal winds  

Navigation becomes increasingly dangerous near the top.

---

### Enemy Types

Possible enemy encounters include:

• Stalker variants  
• Rot-infected birds  
• isolated Rotters trapped inside the structure  

Enemies may appear suddenly in narrow stairways.

---

### Environmental Hazards

Hazards include:

• collapsing floors  
• unstable railings  
• falling debris during storms  

Players must move carefully.

---

### Narrative Elements

At the top of the lighthouse players may discover:

• emergency radio equipment  
• evacuation attempts recorded in logs  
• failed distress transmissions  

This reinforces the feeling that the town attempted to call for help.

---

# 5. Fishing Trawler Graveyard

### Overview

The Trawler Graveyard is a coastal wreck site located near the harbor outskirts.

During the outbreak, many fishing vessels attempted to flee Blackwater Cove.

Several ships ran aground or collided during the chaos.

The result is a cluster of abandoned vessels trapped along the rocky coastline.

The Rot has begun spreading across these ships.

---

### Visual Design

Environmental features include:

• rusted trawlers partially submerged  
• tangled fishing nets  
• broken ship hulls  
• Rot growth covering decks  

The area is covered in fog and crashing waves.

Large ships form a maze-like environment.

---

### Gameplay Role

This area introduces **non-linear navigation across shipwrecks**.

Players may move through:

• broken decks  
• ship interiors  
• cargo holds  
• narrow gangways between vessels  

Vertical navigation becomes important.

---

### Enemy Types

Enemies may include:

• Harbor Rotters  
• Rot Wolves scavenging the wreckage  
• mutated sea creatures  

Enemies may emerge from below deck areas.

---

### Environmental Hazards

Hazards include:

• unstable ship structures  
• flooded cargo areas  
• collapsing walkways  

Players must navigate carefully during combat encounters.

---

### Narrative Elements

Environmental storytelling may reveal:

• evacuation attempts by fishermen  
• abandoned supplies  
• emergency radio messages from ships  

These details show the chaos that occurred during the outbreak.

---

# 6. Setpiece Integration With Districts

Setpieces connect naturally to existing districts.

Example placement:


Harbor District
|
Rot Aquarium
|
Downtown
|
Forest Region
|
Collapsed Lighthouse


The Trawler Graveyard is located along the coastline near the harbor.

These locations extend the playable world while maintaining logical geography.

---

# 7. Setpiece Encounter Design

Setpiece locations may feature special encounter types.

Examples include:

• mini-boss encounters  
• environmental hazards during combat  
• scripted enemy ambushes  
• timed escape sequences  

These encounters should feel unique compared to standard district exploration.

---

# 8. Environmental Storytelling Opportunities

Setpieces should contain rich storytelling details.

Examples:

• abandoned equipment  
• recorded emergency transmissions  
• Halcyon research data  
• personal notes from residents  

These details reinforce the narrative without relying on direct exposition.

---

# 9. Future Setpiece Possibilities

Additional setpieces may be added during development if needed.

Possible ideas include:

• underwater research tunnels  
• abandoned coastal mines  
• collapsed cliffside roads  

However, the initial game should focus on the three primary setpieces defined above.

---

# 10. Design Goals

The goal of setpiece locations is to ensure that ROT contains **visually distinctive environments** that players remember.

These locations should:

• strengthen the world identity  
• expand environmental storytelling  
• create unique gameplay moments  

Together they help make Blackwater Cove feel like a **living place transformed by the Rot** rather than a sequence of similar environments.
