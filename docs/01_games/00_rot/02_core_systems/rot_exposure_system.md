# Rot Exposure System

Project: ROT  
Version: 1.1

Category: Core Mechanics  
Scope: ROT (Game)

---

# 1. Overview

The Rot Exposure System represents the player's biological contamination when interacting with Rot affected environments.

Unlike traditional infection systems, Rot exposure does not immediately turn the player into a monster. Instead, it represents gradual biological contamination caused by prolonged contact with the Rot ecosystem.

Exposure accumulates through environmental contact, creature encounters, and interaction with contaminated materials.

As exposure increases, the player experiences physiological effects that influence gameplay and perception.

The Rot Exposure System introduces a long term survival pressure that encourages careful environmental navigation.

---

# 2. Design Goals

The Rot Exposure System exists to support several gameplay goals.

### Environmental Danger

Rot ecosystems should be dangerous even when enemies are not present.

Exposure ensures that contaminated environments remain threatening.

### Long Term Pressure

Exposure accumulates gradually and cannot always be immediately removed. This creates long term tension during exploration.

### Ecological Integration

The system reinforces the idea that the Rot is a biological ecosystem rather than a traditional infection.

### Player Decision Making

Players must decide how long they can remain in contaminated environments before the effects become dangerous.

---

# 3. Exposure Meter

The player has an internal exposure value that represents their level of Rot contamination.

This value increases when the player interacts with contaminated environments or organisms.

Exposure is represented through a visible meter in the user interface.

The meter typically contains several stages:

- **Stable** – no immediate effects  
- **Contaminated** – minor biological symptoms begin  
- **Critical Exposure** – severe symptoms and gameplay penalties  
- **Mutation Risk** – extreme exposure causing unpredictable effects  

The meter gradually fills as exposure accumulates.

---

# 4. Environmental Exposure

Exposure primarily occurs through environmental contact.

Rot ecosystems release biological particles, spores, and organic compounds that contaminate nearby organisms.

Common environmental exposure sources include:

- dense Rot growth areas  
- contaminated water or organic fluid  
- biological spores in the air  
- heavily mutated terrain  

Different environments produce different exposure rates.

Light contamination areas increase exposure slowly, while dense Rot zones increase exposure rapidly.

---

# 5. Creature Based Exposure

Contact with Rot organisms can also increase exposure.

Examples include:

- physical attacks from Rot creatures  
- contact with biological secretions  
- proximity to certain mutated organisms  

Some organisms may release spores or toxins that temporarily increase exposure levels.

These encounters reinforce the ecological nature of Rot contamination.

---

# 6. Exposure Accumulation

Exposure increases gradually over time.

Several factors influence the accumulation rate:

- time spent in contaminated environments  
- proximity to Rot organisms  
- density of Rot growth in the environment  
- protective equipment worn by the player  

Protective gear can slow exposure but cannot completely prevent it.

This ensures that prolonged exploration in Rot ecosystems always carries risk.

---

# 7. Exposure Effects

As exposure levels increase, the player begins experiencing biological and psychological symptoms.

These effects escalate depending on contamination level.

### Early Symptoms

Early exposure effects may include:

- visual distortions  
- subtle auditory hallucinations  
- reduced stamina recovery  
- environmental discomfort indicators  

These symptoms create tension while remaining manageable.

---

### Intermediate Symptoms

Moderate exposure levels introduce more severe effects.

Examples include:

- increased stamina consumption  
- reduced movement efficiency  
- stronger visual distortion  
- increased vulnerability to Rot organisms  

These symptoms begin to influence gameplay decisions.

---

### Severe Exposure

At high exposure levels, the player experiences significant biological stress.

Possible effects include:

- severe visual disturbance  
- difficulty maintaining stamina  
- temporary loss of control during mutation events  
- increased risk of permanent contamination

High exposure creates immediate pressure to escape contaminated environments.

---

# 8. Mutation Effects

Extreme exposure may trigger temporary mutation effects.

These effects represent the Rot attempting to restructure the player's biology.

Mutation effects are unpredictable and may include:

- temporary sensory distortions  
- biological growth on the player's body  
- sudden bursts of physical instability  
- altered perception of nearby organisms

Mutation events reinforce the biological horror theme of the game.

These effects are temporary but dangerous.

---

# 9. Cleansing Mechanics

Players can reduce exposure through several cleansing methods.

Examples include:

### Decontamination Stations

Research facilities may contain equipment capable of partially cleansing Rot contamination.

### Medical Treatment

Certain crafted or discovered medical compounds may reduce exposure levels.

### Safe Zones

Areas free from Rot contamination allow the player to gradually recover from mild exposure.

Recovery in safe zones is slow but consistent.

---

# 10. Exposure Risk Management

Players must balance exploration with exposure risk.

Staying too long in contaminated environments increases biological stress.

Managing exposure becomes a strategic decision.

Players may choose to:

- retreat to safe areas  
- use cleansing items  
- avoid highly contaminated regions  
- acquire protective equipment  

This system reinforces survival decision making.

---

# 11. Integration with Other Systems

The Rot Exposure System interacts with several gameplay systems.

Examples include:

- **environmental design**, which determines contamination zones  
- **enemy design**, where creatures increase exposure risk  
- **inventory and equipment systems**, which provide protective gear  
- **narrative systems**, where research documents explain Rot contamination  

These interactions ensure that exposure remains deeply integrated into the overall gameplay experience.

---

# 12. Purpose of This Document

This document defines how Rot contamination affects the player.

The Rot Exposure System reinforces the ecological horror identity of ROT by turning the environment itself into a persistent threat.

By managing exposure levels, players must carefully navigate contaminated ecosystems while uncovering the mysteries of the Rot.
